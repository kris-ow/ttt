This video from brighterwithherbert delivers a comprehensive global status report on Tesla's Full Self-Driving rollout, arguing that the technology has crossed a critical inflection point — moving from a long-promised feature to an actively deployed, multi-country platform. The host contends that a major narrative shift is underway among mainstream media and former skeptics, and that April 2026 represents a convergence of catalysts that could trigger a significant market re-rating of Tesla's autonomous driving value. ## Autonomous Driving

1. **Global FSD Supervised Deployment Status:** FSD Supervised is currently live and available to customers in seven countries: the United States (live since 2023), Canada, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea (launched November 2025). South Korea has already logged 5 million FSD miles in just 100 days, with the Korea Expressway Corporation rating highway performance as "excellent." China holds partial approval but remains constrained by data localization laws — Tesla cannot transfer training videos outside China, and the government banned the term "Full Self-Driving," requiring Tesla to rename it "intelligent assisted driving." Elon Musk has said he expects full approval in China in 2026.

2. **Unsupervised FSD — Austin Geofenced Deployment:** Unsupervised FSD, requiring no driver, is currently active in geofenced areas of Austin. The host notes approximately 14 reported incidents over 18 months in the Austin robotaxi service and acknowledges that fully unsupervised operation remains limited to small geofenced boundaries in just one city as of the video's publish date.

3. **Netherlands Approval — The EU Gateway Catalyst:** The Dutch Vehicle Authority (RDW) has been running an 18-month testing program with Tesla, during which Tesla completed 1.66 million km of testing on European roads and conducted over 13,000 customer ride-alongs. All documentation for UN Regulation 171 approval and Article 39 exemptions has been submitted. The RDW confirmed in late March that they are in the final internal review stage, with a decision expected on April 10, 2026. Under EU Regulation 2018/858, a Netherlands type approval could trigger mutual recognition across all 27 EU member states, with Tesla anticipating a coordinated EU-wide rollout by summer 2026. FSD demonstration ride-alongs across Germany, France, Italy, Denmark, and Switzerland ran through March 31st and were extended multiple times due to popularity.

4. **Other Pending Markets — Israel, Japan, Abu Dhabi:** Israel's Transport Minister Mirie Regev publicly stated FSD is "coming soon," following approval of supervised autonomous driving trials on public roads in February 2026. Japan's Tesla President Hiroshi Hashimoto confirmed FSD is targeting a launch in Japan in 2026, with testing actively underway in Tokyo using both Model Y and Model 3 vehicles; an over-the-air update could instantly activate FSD for approximately 40,000 Teslas already on Japanese roads. Abu Dhabi launched supervised FSD trials on public roads on March 1st under the Abu Dhabi Mobility Authority — described as structured trials, not yet a full consumer launch, but representing the first confirmed FSD footprint in the Middle East.

5. **FSD Version 14.3 — "The Last Big Piece of the Puzzle":** Elon Musk confirmed on March 19th that FSD version 14.3 is currently in testing, with a wide release expected in early to mid-April 2026. Elon described this version as where "the last big piece of the puzzle lands," adding an order-of-magnitude larger neural network, more reasoning capabilities, and reinforcement learning. The host quotes Elon saying the car could feel "almost like a sentient being" after this update.

6. **Pedestrian Intent Prediction — Technical Breakthrough:** A viral clip shared through Tesla's official account showed FSD slowing and yielding for a pedestrian who was still standing on the sidewalk — not yet moving toward the road — and the pedestrian then stepped out seconds later exactly as the AI anticipated. The host explains this is possible because FSD version 14's end-to-end neural network unifies perception, prediction, and planning into a single architecture rather than separate modules, enabling the system to read body language cues such as orientation, head direction, and weight shift to model intent before action. The host argues this is the kind of pedestrian-safety capability regulators are specifically looking for in approval processes.

7. **Safety Data and Elon's Projection:** Tesla's Q2 2025 safety report showed FSD Supervised vehicles were involved in approximately one crash every 7.6 million miles driven, compared to the national average of roughly one crash per 670,000 miles for human drivers — placing current FSD at approximately seven times safer on a per-mile basis. Elon posted on X that Tesla AI self-driving "will be greater than 10 times safer than human driving," which the host carefully clarifies is a forward-looking projection, not a confirmed current statistic. The host also acknowledges limitations: Tesla's safety data is self-reported and not independently verified, the comparison methodology has legitimate critiques, and FSD may operate more frequently in favorable conditions.

8. **Tesla's Cumulative Training Data Advantage:** The host states Tesla has accumulated 8.4 billion miles of training data — described as more than all other autonomous driving programs combined — giving Tesla a compounding data advantage as each deployment generates further training material.

9. **Narrative Shift Among Media and Industry:** MotorTrend named FSD Supervised the winner of their Best Driver Assistance System award for 2026, with editors explicitly noting that "the majority of the flaws we noted in earlier versions have largely been addressed." Germany's largest car magazine called FSD "genuinely stunning" after years of skepticism. At CES 2026, industry analysts noted the entire autonomous driving industry has converged on Tesla-like vision-based solutions, with one analyst calling it "an admission that alternative paths were dead ends."
