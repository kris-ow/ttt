This Munro Live video features Paul Turnbull and Matthew Lawrence conducting a detailed engineering teardown of a BYD electric drive unit (the IDM210) manufactured by BorgWarner for BYD. The video's core thesis is that this motor represents a new cost and performance benchmark for electric drive units in the over-200-horsepower class, while also identifying one significant engineering flaw in the stator's flexible lead connections. The analysis covers the gearbox, inverter, rotor, stator, and cooling systems, comparing BYD's design choices against industry peers including Tesla, GM, Hyundai, Ford, and Toyota.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Motor Specifications and Applications:** The BYD IDM210 motor produces 183 kW and approximately 330 Nm of torque, weighing 84 kg. The "210" designation refers to the stator's internal diameter in millimeters. It is part of a new family of BYD motors and is used as the rear drive unit in the Han (performance sedan), Tang (SUV), and Tang L (seven-passenger SUV). A higher-performance version of the motor in this family reportedly produces close to 500 kW, and the family spans a range from under approximately 150 kW up to 350 kW or more. The same stator layout is also used in the BYD Shark, where an eight-pole version is employed.

2. **BorgWarner as Supplier:** Despite being a BYD product, BorgWarner branding is prominently displayed throughout the unit, indicating BorgWarner manufactured this drive unit to BYD's specifications. The analysts note that BYD customizes specifications for the China market — for example, opting for water cooling rather than oil cooling — in ways that differ from what BorgWarner might offer North American or European customers.

3. **Gearbox Design:** The drive unit uses a two-stage gear reduction with an unusually high gear ratio of 12.83:1, enabling the motor to spin at close to 18,000 RPM. The three-gear arrangement (input shaft, layshaft, and differential) is described as extremely compact given the torque loads involved. Oil lubrication is achieved passively by the differential's outer gear slinging oil into pockets at the top of the casting, which then drains through channels to lubricate all bearings — no dedicated oil pump is required.

4. **Inverter Design — Novel Switching Package:** The inverter is a thin, flat "pancake" design mounted on top of the motor to preserve rear cargo space. It uses 12 discrete switching devices (in pairs, six per phase), cooled on both sides through copper cooling channels. The switching devices are only four millimeters thick, which the analysts describe as a step above anything they have previously seen in the industry, likely using bare-die mounting directly onto the substrate. The dual-sided copper cooling also acts as electromagnetic shielding, protecting sensitive gate-drive and current-sensing electronics from the switching noise. The analysts note that during full-throttle acceleration, the power passing through these switches is equivalent to that consumed by 15 to 20 homes.

5. **Current Sensors — Compact and Cost-Effective:** The motor uses three current sensors rather than the two used by Tesla. The analysts note that while only two are mathematically needed for motor control, a third enables fault detection. Uniquely, BYD's current sensors operate without the typical steel yoke shielding, made possible by the fully shielded switching package — saving cost and reducing package size significantly.

6. **Innovative Friction-Stir-Welded Water Cooling Jacket:** To address historical leak problems seen in an earlier BYD motor teardown, the IDM210 uses an inner and outer cast aluminum housing friction-stir-welded together, then machined to a seamless, hermetically sealed surface. This eliminates O-rings and RTV seals entirely. The analysts call this a "bulletproof" solution for the China market, where water cooling is preferred over oil cooling. They note that in the China market, the use of heavy rare earth elements (dysprosium and terbium) in the magnets mitigates the rotor heating risks associated with water cooling, whereas Western markets face supply chain constraints on heavy rare earths that make oil cooling more appropriate.

7. **Rotor Design — Unique OD Groove:** The rotor is an interior permanent magnet machine with a double-V magnet construction. Notably, it features a deep groove on the outside diameter — something the analysts say they have not seen on any other permanent magnet rotor. Spinning at close to 20,000 RPM, this groove required detailed finite element analysis to manage hoop stress. The rotor diameter is approximately 138 mm, slightly smaller than the industry-typical 150 mm (~6 inches), which helps reduce centrifugal stress. The groove flattens the magnetic flux profile from a prominent spike to a more sinusoidal waveform, reducing motor losses and torque ripple.

8. **Bearing Current Mitigation — Cost-Effective Solution:** The rear bearing uses a steel insert coated with a low-electrical-conductivity material to electrically isolate the bearing from shaft-induced voltages, combined with a carbon fiber brush on the shaft to provide a low-resistance discharge path. The analysts explain this is significantly cheaper than using ceramic ball bearings or other methods while fully eliminating bearing wear from bearing currents.

9. **Stator Design — Benchmark Bar Winding with One Critical Flaw:** The stator uses a 72-slot, six-pole, three-parallel-path bar winding design — the same topology used in the Chevrolet Bolt (which was 72-slot, eight-pole). The analysts praise the minimization of special conductors and the balanced inductance across all three parallel paths. However, they identify a serious engineering concern: the flexible stranded-wire leads connecting the stator to the inverter are mechanically unsupported at the stator end. The leads are welded at both ends and carry hundreds of amps, meaning thermal expansion and multi-source vibration (road, rotor magnetic fields, switching) continuously flex the weld joint. Copper optimized for conductivity is a poor structural material, creating fatigue crack risk over time. The proposed fix is a small plastic support under the free end of the lead — analogous to a plastic block already used at the inverter end.

10. **Overall Cost Benchmark:** The analysts conclude that, taken as a whole, this is very likely the lowest-cost drive unit Munro has ever encountered for this capability class (over 200 horsepower). They note BYD is already returning to BorgWarner seeking further cost reductions despite this unit setting a new industry benchmark, and predict that technology from this motor will propagate broadly across the automotive industry.

## Market & Competition

1. **BYD's Competitive Position:** The video frames BYD as the dominant player in China's EV and hybrid market and notes significant concern among European and US automakers about BYD's technological and cost trajectory. The motor's estimated production volume is approximately 150,000 units, with the analysts noting the actual volume may be higher given its use across multiple vehicle platforms.

2. **Industry Convergence on Drive Unit Architecture:** The analysts observe a broad industry convergence on the same basic electric drive unit architecture: two-stage gear reduction, bar-wound stator, interior permanent magnet rotor with single or double-V magnet construction, and an integrated inverter in a single housing. Toyota, GM (Bolt), Hyundai, Ford, Nissan, and others are all noted as using variations of this approach. BYD and BorgWarner's IDM210 is seen as the current cost-efficiency leader within this converging design space.
