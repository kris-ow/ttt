This episode of "brighterwithherbert" covers several breaking Tesla updates, including the tape-out of Tesla's AI5 chip, the broader rollout of FSD version 14.3.1, new Tesla Insurance safety score incentives tied to FSD usage, robotaxi hiring signals in Dallas and Houston, and the possibility of Giga Shanghai producing Optimus humanoid robots. The video's core thesis is that Tesla's chip team has leapfrogged competitors like Nvidia in efficiency and cost, AI4 is already sufficient for unsupervised FSD deployment, and multiple converging signals point toward an imminent robotaxi expansion. Host Herbert is joined by Tesla journalist Brian White (channel: Futuraza), who is on-site near Lathrop, California for a Megapack factory tour.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Version 14.3.1 Rolls Out Broadly with Strong Early Reception:** A new point release, FSD version 14.3.1, began rolling out not just to early testers but more broadly, including to users in Canada and to Cybertruck owners. Early users describe it as a significant jump even over 14.3, which itself had featured 20% faster reaction time without requiring 10x parameters. Reviewer Zach noted improvements including better lane bias on highways, a new parking icon ("P icon") shown consistently at destinations with selectable parking spots, smoother stop sign behavior, improved speed bump handling, better gated parking lot navigation, more decisive parking spot selection, and Grok integration requiring zero input. Another reviewer noted FSD 14.3.1 did not exceed 83 mph, calling it more conservative and ready for wide release.

2. **Tesla Insurance Safety Score 3.0 Rewards FSD Supervised Driving:** Tesla announced a new Safety Score version 3.0 under which every mile driven with FSD supervised enabled automatically receives a score of 100, allowing drivers to maintain a higher average safety score and lower monthly insurance premiums. This currently applies to new policies in Indiana, Tennessee, Texas, Arizona, Virginia, and Illinois. The hosts noted that in Washington state, a partnership with Lemonade insurance offers a 50% discount for miles driven using FSD supervised. The hosts framed this as both a financial incentive for adoption and a vote of confidence by Tesla in its own safety system — noting that for some customers, the insurance savings could exceed the $99 FSD subscription fee, making FSD effectively free or net positive financially.

3. **AI4 Deemed Sufficient for Unsupervised FSD Deployment:** Elon Musk clarified that AI4 is already enough to achieve better-than-human safety for FSD, and that AI5 is targeted at Optimus and supercomputer clusters rather than being a prerequisite for cars. The hosts interpret this as a strong signal that Tesla does not need to wait for AI5 availability (estimated by the hosts to be no earlier than the second half of 2027) to achieve unsupervised FSD rollout, and that FSD 14.3's architecture was optimized to maximize what AI4 can deliver.

## Robotaxi

1. **Tesla Hiring Robotaxi Field Response Specialists in Dallas and Houston:** Tesla is actively hiring robotaxi field response specialists covering 24/7 shifts (day, afternoon, and night) in Dallas and Houston, which the hosts describe as the clearest signal yet of imminent robotaxi expansion to those cities. The hosts noted that Tesla is also reportedly hiring for robotaxi-related roles across approximately 34 cities, suggesting a broad operational buildout underway.

2. **No New NHTSA-Reported Incidents Since February 15th:** According to robotaxi tracker data cited in the video, Tesla's Austin robotaxi operation reported five incidents (all fender benders, one at 17 mph, the rest under 4 mph) between mid-December and mid-January, one incident between mid-January and mid-February, and zero reported incidents from February 15th onward through the date of this video (April 15, 2026). The hosts emphasized this zero-incident streak is occurring with a higher number of vehicles deployed than in December, making it an even stronger data point.

3. **Cybercab Units Spotted at Giga Texas in Large Numbers:** Journalist Joe Tegtmeyer shared photos from Giga Texas showing multiple groups of Cybercab units, some actively navigating a figure-8 test track, others lined up for transport or processing. The hosts cited this visible production volume as strong evidence that Cybercab is not years away, drawing parallels to early sightings of Model 3s in Shanghai and Cybertrucks, which preceded full ramp-ups.

4. **Robotaxi Expansion Pace Explanation Sought for Q1 Earnings:** The hosts noted that Tesla had previously said it expected to double its robotaxi fleet per month and that this did not happen. They flagged the upcoming Q1 2026 earnings call (scheduled for April 22, 2026) as an opportunity for Tesla to explain the bottleneck — whether safety, operations, hardware, or software — and clarify whether they are now ready to scale.

## Humanoid Bots

1. **AI5 Chip Taped Out — Targeted at Optimus and Supercomputer Clusters:** Elon Musk announced that Tesla's AI chip team has completed the tape-out of the AI5 chip, with work now beginning on AI6, Dojo 3, and other chips. Elon confirmed that AI5 is intended for Optimus and Tesla's supercomputer clusters. A single AI5 delivers five times the useful compute of a dual AI4 setup, with eight times compute power, nine times memory, and five times bandwidth. Notably, AI5 is approximately three times more power efficient than Nvidia's Blackwell GPU and costs under 10% of Blackwell's cost. The chip uses an integer-based inference system rather than floating-point, which is more power and silicon efficient, and is purpose-built for Tesla's AI software stack rather than general-purpose workloads.

2. **Elon's Personal Commitment to AI5:** Elon noted that he worked on this chip "every weekend without exception for six months," underscoring its strategic importance. The tape-out milestone was described as analogous to "picture lock" in filmmaking — the point at which the design is finalized before manufacturing begins. The hosts noted that AI5 was somewhat delayed relative to earlier expectations.

3. **Giga Shanghai Floated as Potential Optimus Production Site:** Tesla China President Alan Wang Hao publicly stated that Giga Shanghai has the potential to produce humanoid robots in the future, calling the factory a possible "golden key" to mass production of robots and citing its manufacturing efficiency. This is described as the first time a senior Tesla executive has publicly mentioned Shanghai's potential role in Optimus manufacturing. Brian White noted that there is no existing idle space inside the current Giga Shanghai footprint for robot production, but suggested Tesla could build a new adjacent facility, as Tesla holds an option on neighboring land and operates other facilities in the district. The hosts discussed geopolitical implications — robots built in China would likely face U.S. national security scrutiny, while robots built in the U.S. would face equivalent questions in China — suggesting Tesla may ultimately run parallel production lines for each market.

4. **Terafab Hiring Underway Faster Than Expected:** Tesla is actively hiring for Terafab roles including senior process engineers (wet and dry), dielectric and metal deposition specialists, integration engineers, plating engineers, and metrology experts. The hosts expressed surprise at the pace of hiring, having expected Terafab to be three to five years away, and interpreted the active job postings as a signal that the program is being accelerated.

## Market & Competition

1. **Tesla's Custom Chip Strategy vs. Nvidia's General-Purpose Approach:** The hosts highlighted Tesla's structural advantage in chip design: by building chips specifically for its own AI software stack (using integer-based inference rather than floating-point), Tesla can dramatically simplify and optimize the silicon in ways Nvidia cannot, since Nvidia must serve a broad range of customers and legacy software compatibility. This is framed as a key reason AI5 achieves Nvidia Blackwell-comparable performance for Tesla workloads at under 10% of the cost and three times the power efficiency. The hosts contrasted this with competitors like Rivian and Lucid, which must rely on off-the-shelf solutions that aren't optimized for their specific needs.
