Registration data from S&P Global Mobility confirms that SpaceX purchased 1,279 Cybertrucks in Q4 2025 — 18% of all US Cybertruck registrations that quarter — propping up sales figures that would otherwise have fallen 51% year-over-year.

## Electric Vehicles
1. **Scale of inter-company purchases:** Of 7,071 Cybertrucks registered in the US in Q4 2025, SpaceX accounted for 1,279 units. An additional 60 units went to xAI, The Boring Company, and Neuralink, bringing the total Musk-entity share to 1,339 vehicles, or roughly 19% of Q4 registrations. Purchases continued into 2026: Musk's companies bought another 158 Cybertrucks in January 2026 and 67 in February 2026.

2. **Underlying demand collapse:** Stripping out inter-company purchases, the Cybertruck's organic sales pace is approximately 20,000 units per year — less than 10% of the 250,000 annual figure Tesla originally projected. The Ford F-150 Lightning outsold the Cybertruck in 2025 before Ford discontinued the Lightning for insufficient demand. Sam Fiorani of AutoForecast Solutions stated: "Tesla is running out of buyers for the Cybertruck."

3. **$60,000 AWD variant as demand test:** On February 19, 2026, Tesla launched a new AWD Cybertruck at $60,000 — the lowest price ever offered — and cut the Cyberbeast by $15,000 to $100,000. Q4 2025 through February 2026 data predates this launch, meaning Q2 2026 will be the first full quarter reflecting consumer response to the lower price point.

## Financials
1. **Revenue implications:** At Q4 2025 Cybertruck pricing, the 1,339 Musk-entity purchases represent well over $100 million in revenue Tesla booked by selling to companies Elon Musk controls. SpaceX is not publicly traded, so these fleet purchases are not disclosed the way deals with public fleet operators would be.

## Editorial Stance
The author argues the SpaceX purchases allowed Tesla to obscure a severe demand failure, raises governance concerns about a public company (Tesla) booking revenue from a private company (SpaceX) controlled by the same individual, and frames Q2 2026 as the decisive test of whether the $60,000 AWD Cybertruck can generate genuine consumer demand.
