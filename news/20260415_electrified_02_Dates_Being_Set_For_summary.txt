This episode of Electrified, hosted by Dillon Loomis, covers a wide range of Tesla developments centered on the accelerating global rollout of FSD, expanding Cybercab testing, EU regulatory momentum, and the broader competitive landscape for EVs and autonomous vehicles. The core thesis is that Tesla's supervised FSD is gaining unprecedented global momentum — particularly in Europe — while Cybercab testing continues to expand nationwide ahead of a robotaxi network launch. The video also touches on Optimus manufacturing prospects, U.S. EV market data, Cybertruck sales struggles, and a compelling safety case for autonomous driving.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Version 14.3.1 Rolling Out to Early Access Group:** Just 7 days after FSD version 14.3 rolled out to the early access (EA) group, version 14.3.1 has also been pushed to that same group. Reviewer "Sawyer" noted improvements including a new tap-on-parking-icon UI, a more conservative MadMax highway feel, and resolution of brake stabbing issues. Remaining concerns include navigation routes and occasional parking alignment. The host sees this as a likely candidate for the next wide release, with an FSD-specific in-car app also announced as part of Tesla's spring software update — currently limited to AI4 vehicles — featuring a new dashboard, FSD usage tracking, streaks, and tutorials.

2. **EU-Wide FSD Approval Process Advancing:** The Dutch Vehicle Authority (RDW) has officially notified the European Commission of its plan to seek EU-wide approval for Tesla's supervised FSD. The RDW's GM expressed confidence that FSD can be used safely on all roads in the Netherlands, including Amsterdam, and stated that if it's good enough for the Netherlands, it's good enough for Europe. The case will be presented to the Technical Committee of Motor Vehicles (TCMV) — the next meeting of which is May 5, 2026 — where a vote requires approval from at least 55% of the 27 member states (15 states) representing at least 65% of the total EU population. If the May 5th vote doesn't occur, the next opportunity is June 30, 2026. Even if the EU-wide vote fails, individual member states may adopt the RDW approval independently, and non-EU countries such as Norway, the UK, Iceland, Switzerland, Turkey, Japan, India, and Thailand may also recognize the approval.

3. **European FSD Differs from U.S. Version:** Elon Musk confirmed the European version is "basically version 14.3" but noted regulatory differences: stricter driver attention monitoring, a requirement that significant software updates be pre-checked with the RDW (similar to China's requirements), and the use of a max speed offset feature (scroll wheel speed adjustment) rather than speed profiles — a feature that was recently removed for U.S. users. Early European user reviews praise FSD's smoothness and safety feel but flag issues with lane selection, sign comprehension, speed control, and hesitation, consistent with early-stage deployment.

4. **Potential EU Fleet and Take Rate:** Estimating the AI4-equipped Tesla fleet across EU countries plus likely RDW-follower nations (Norway, UK, Switzerland, Iceland, Turkey), approximately 30% of Tesla's fleet in those markets is AI4-compatible, yielding a midpoint estimate of roughly 450,000 eligible vehicles. A take rate of 15–20% is considered reasonable given current social media momentum, potentially adding hundreds of thousands of active FSD users and generating significant new training data — notably, unlike Chinese data, European data can improve the global FSD model.

5. **Safety Case for Autonomous Driving — Estimated 200,000 Lives Saved Over 10 Years:** Dillon Loomis analyzed NHTSA FARS data over the past decade, finding over 389,000 total U.S. traffic fatalities. After conservatively adjusting for overlap across behavioral categories — alcohol, distraction, speeding, and drowsiness — he estimates that a Level 4 or 5 autonomous system like supervised FSD could have prevented between 198,000 and 215,000 deaths over that period, or roughly 20,000 lives per year. Additionally, distraction alone causes approximately 250,000 injuries annually in the U.S., a number that autonomous driving would substantially reduce.

6. **Tesla Insurance Safety Score Update:** Tesla Insurance's Safety Score version 3.0 now awards a score of 100 for every mile driven with supervised FSD enabled, supporting higher average scores and lower monthly premiums. This currently applies only to new policies in Indiana, Tennessee, Texas, Arizona, Virginia, and Illinois. Questions remain about existing policies.

## Robotaxi

1. **Cybercab Testing Now Spans 10 States Plus Washington D.C.:** Cybercab on-road testing has been confirmed in Alaska, California, Nevada, Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas, Illinois, Florida, Washington D.C., New York, and Massachusetts — 10 states plus D.C. Two Cybercabs were spotted in Naples, Florida on the day of recording. The host notes this count excludes pop-up displays and includes only confirmed road testing.

2. **Tesla Hiring Data Collection Supervisors Across New States:** Tesla's careers page lists 34 data collection supervisor roles for self-driving (plus 1 for Optimus), with new positions in states where autonomous testing has not yet been widely seen, including Pennsylvania, Missouri, Minnesota, Louisiana, Maryland, New Jersey, Georgia, Tennessee, and Michigan. The host speculates these roles will likely involve Cybercab vehicles equipped with steering wheels for supervised data gathering.

3. **Austin Unsupervised Robotaxi Fleet Grows:** The robotaxi tracker now counts 11 unsupervised vehicles operating in Austin, up from 9 previously. The host also clarified that claims of zero robotaxi incidents since January are technically accurate per available data, but NHTSA data only extends to February 17, 2026, with a new monthly report expected imminently that should cover through March.

## Humanoid Bots

1. **Giga Shanghai Flagged as Potential Optimus Production Site:** The President of Tesla China stated that Giga Shanghai has the potential to build Optimus in the future, calling it a possible "golden key to the mass production of robots." He expressed confidence in welcoming a new era of robots but did not clarify whether production would occur in existing Shanghai facilities or a new plant. The host notes Elon Musk has previously acknowledged China as Tesla's toughest humanoid robot competitor due to its manufacturing and AI strengths, but also said Optimus will be more capable in real-world intelligence and electromechanical dexterity — particularly hand design. The host's personal estimate is that Optimus production at Giga Shanghai is unlikely before late 2027 or into 2028.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Tesla U.S. EV Market Share Surges to 54.2%:** Cox Automotive Q1 2026 data shows Tesla's U.S. EV market share rose from 43.2% in Q1 2025 to 54.2% in Q1 2026 — back above 50%. However, this is primarily driven by broad EV industry contraction rather than Tesla sales growth, as Tesla's own unit sales were down 8% year-over-year. Total U.S. EV sales for Q1 2026 were down 27% year-over-year. Only one non-Tesla EV exceeded 10,000 units for the quarter: the Toyota BZ4X at just over 10,029 units.

2. **Cybertruck Sales Down 45% Year-Over-Year:** Cybertruck sold 3,519 units in Q1 2026 vs. 6,460 units in Q1 2025 — a 45% decline. It remained the best-selling EV truck in the U.S. but against a low bar. The host notes the lower-priced Cybertruck variant won't impact sales figures until Q2 2026 at the earliest, possibly Q3 depending on delivery timing.

3. **Model S/X Signature Edition Announced:** Tesla revealed a farewell Signature Edition of the Model S and Model X, limited to just 350 total units, available by invite only and not orderable via the configurator. Featured in a distinctive garnet red with gold brake calipers.

4. **Tesla Semi Debuts at Canadian Truck World:** Canadians will get their first in-person look at the Tesla Semi at Truck World, held at the International Centre in Mississauga, Ontario. The Semi will be part of a ride-and-drive experience, targeting trucking executives, fleet operators, and government stakeholders.

## Market & Competition

1. **Legacy Automakers Signal Existential Crisis Over Chinese Competition:** Honda's CEO returned from a China visit saying "we have no chance against this," and the company has cancelled two Zero-series EVs (SUV and sedan), an Acura RSX revival, and both Sony Afeela EV co-developments, while booking a nearly $16 billion loss on EV write-offs. Honda's China sales have fallen from approximately 1.6 million in 2020 to 640,000 in 2025 — roughly halved — and only about 50% of its manufacturing capacity is being utilized versus the 70–80% needed for profitability. Former Toyota CEO Akio Toyoda warned suppliers "unless things change, we will not survive." Ford CEO Jim Farley stated that Chinese automakers entering the U.S. market would be "devastating" to American manufacturing, citing state subsidies as creating an unfair competitive advantage.
