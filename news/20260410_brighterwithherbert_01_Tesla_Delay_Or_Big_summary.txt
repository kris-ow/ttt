This video is a roundtable discussion hosted by "brighterwithherbert" featuring James from Invest Answers, Alexandra Merz, and Jeff Lutz, debating whether Tesla is experiencing meaningful delays or is on the cusp of major milestones. The panel covers FSD version 14.3, robotaxi deployment progress, Cybercab production, the speculative Tesla-SpaceX merger thesis, and broader market sentiment. The core argument is that while some milestones have slipped versus Q4 2025 guidance, the underlying signals — manufacturing ramp, city deployments, software rewrites — point toward imminent progress rather than structural delay.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD 14.3 Released — A Complete Rewrite, Not an Incremental Update:** FSD version 14.3 was released on Tuesday (approximately on time relative to Elon's end-of-week target), and the panel describes it as a ground-up rewrite of the AI compiler and runtime — not a minor update. The prior AI system was described as having been "dumbed down" because it couldn't fully leverage the Hardware 4 AI chip. Version 14.3 rebuilds the entire system to properly utilize that hardware, delivering a reported 20% improvement in reaction time speed. Early testers (estimated 50–100 people) are giving it strong reviews, describing it as smoother, faster, and more responsive.

2. **Version 15 Debate — 10x Parameter Model Deferred:** Elon posted that FSD version 15 — not 14.3 — will be the "large model" 10x parameter version that he says will "far exceed human levels of safety even in completely unsupervised and complex situations." This triggered community debate about whether robotaxi deployment is now delayed pending version 15. The panel pushes back, arguing that the small model used in version 14.3 has iterated faster than the large model, that version 15 has no specific date attached to it, and that version 14.3 is the platform on which unsupervised robotaxi expansion will proceed. Jeff Lutz notes that "banish" and other previously discussed features are objectively absent from 14.3, acknowledging valid concerns while maintaining that unsupervised FSD for personal vehicles remains the key near-term milestone.

3. **FSD Expansion to Europe — Netherlands Approval Pending:** The panel discusses a live poll showing 58% of viewers expect FSD approval in the Netherlands as of April 10, 2026 (the video's publish date). Alexandra Merz notes that some European users attempted to circumvent restrictions and had their FSD capability remotely deactivated by Tesla, illustrating Tesla's cautious regulatory approach. Full pin-to-pin FSD is described as available only in North America, Korea, and Australia at the time of recording. UK and German customers can purchase a full FSD package for approximately £8,000 / €8,000, but that package does not include address-to-address navigation.

4. **Version 14 Light for Hardware 3 Vehicles:** The panel notes (attributed to Cern) that the version 14.3 rewrite also enables a "version 14 light" to be made available for older Model 3 Hardware 3 vehicles, significantly expanding the addressable user base for the updated system.

## Robotaxi

1. **Austin and Bay Area Fleet — Safety Record and Expansion Signals:** The panel references NHTSA reports showing a strong safety record for Tesla's supervised robotaxi fleet operating in Austin and the Bay Area. Incident data cited: multiple fender-bender-only cases in December 2025–January 2026, declining to one low-speed case in the January 15–February 15 window. Jeff Lutz emphasizes that Tesla has been moving Model Y robotaxi vehicles equipped with Texas and California plates to five or six new U.S. cities, and that job requisitions for robotaxi operations staff continue to grow in quantity and variety across the United States — which he interprets as strong evidence the deployment plan remains on track.

2. **Unsupervised Expansion as the Key Near-Term Milestone:** The panel identifies the transition from supervised to unsupervised robotaxi operation — specifically in Austin and eventually the Bay Area — as the single most important near-term milestone, more important than earnings or volume deliveries. Jeff Lutz argues this will "go viral," change investor behavior, and potentially move the stock. The question he says should be asked at the earnings call is: what is Tesla's current understanding of when unsupervised FSD and unsupervised robotaxis will be released?

3. **Robotaxi Unit Economics — The Core Bull Case:** James presents a slide comparing Tesla's current car business economics to the robotaxi model. A sold car generates approximately $3,000 in one-time profit with an average holding period of about six years. A single Cybercab robotaxi is described as expected to generate $30,000–$50,000 in recurring annual profit. The cost to manufacture a Cybercab is cited at $15,000, though Jeff Lutz estimates the terminal production cost will likely settle between $18,000–$20,000 after at least a year of ramp. The panel describes this as a fundamental business model transformation from "shifting iron" to deploying income-generating robots.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Q1 2026 Deliveries — Inventory Spike Explained by Transit:** The panel addresses concern over Tesla's Q1 2026 delivery miss (described as within 1–2% of consensus for car deliveries, but with energy deliveries significantly below consensus). James highlights that China had its largest-ever export month in March 2026, with approximately 30,000 units, which he argues explains most or all of the apparent inventory spike. Jeff Lutz adds that Tesla's days of inventory moved from roughly 14–15 days in Q4 2025 to approximately 28–29 days in Q1 2026 — a pattern he says is similar to the same quarter transition in prior years and is not a demand signal, as 40–50% of that inventory is in transit.

2. **Model Q — Reported Development in China:** Reuters and Bloomberg reportedly confirmed that Tesla has contacted suppliers to build parts for a low-cost vehicle referred to as "Model Q," being developed in China. The panel is cautious about the sourcing (unnamed, unsourced), but Jeff Lutz notes that if development is genuinely underway in China, Tesla could ship the vehicle as early as next year (2027) given China's rapid product development cadence. He suggests the Model Q will likely reuse Cybercab parts to reduce complexity.

3. **BEV Sales Uptick in Europe — Oil Price Correlation:** James notes an early data signal showing a significant uptick in battery electric vehicle sales in Europe in March 2026. He also presents a chart showing an inverse correlation between oil prices and Tesla's stock price — as oil prices decline, Tesla's stock historically rises — framing lower oil as a potential tailwind for EV demand.

## Humanoid Bots

1. **Optimus Gen 3 Sightings — "Creepy" and "Sublime":** James mentions that he has personally met two people who have seen Optimus Gen 3 under NDA, with one describing it as "creepy" and the other as "sublime." He does not provide technical specifics but says "what's coming will blow your mind" and contrasts it favorably against Chinese robot demonstrations focused on dancing or physical stunts.

2. **Terafab Partnership with Intel — Chip Manufacturing Synergy:** James briefly highlights the Terafab partnership with Intel, describing it as having no negatives he can identify. Intel's access to lithographic machines and chip fabrication infrastructure in space is cited as uniquely complementary to Elon's framework of accelerated fabrication and co-located production. Jeff Lutz elaborates that the key differentiators in Elon's construct are getting to production with high-complexity parts faster and doing so under one roof — and that Elon has historically been accurate at identifying bottlenecks two, five, and ten years in advance.

## Market & Competition

1. **Technical Analysis — Most Oversold Since April 2025:** James presents a historical price action chart showing Tesla is currently at its most oversold level since the April 2025 tariff selloff, when the stock fell to $200. He notes Tesla's average return from January 1 to April 7 going back to 2026 is down approximately 17%, with the current year down approximately 20.84%, consistent with a historically weak first 90–100 days pattern. He calls it the best buying opportunity in a year on a relative technical basis, noting that even in strong years like 2020 (up 730% full year), the first 100 days were only up 27%.

2. **Tesla-SpaceX Merger Thesis:** Alexandra Merz provides an extensive breakdown of her merger thesis. She believes SpaceX will acquire Tesla at equal market caps — with SpaceX targeting an IPO at approximately $1.75 trillion (not $2 trillion, citing Elon's dismissal of a Bloomberg report citing $2 trillion). She expects the merger announcement to come within three to six weeks of the SpaceX IPO, which she expects to file a public S-1 in May 2026. She outlines a timeline in which shareholder votes could conclude by late October to mid-November 2026, with regulatory approval taking six to nine months thereafter, meaning the Tesla ticker could disappear and be replaced by the combined SpaceX entity somewhere between April and June 2027. She argues institutional investors (Vanguard at ~9% voting power, BlackRock, State Street) will require a premium to approve the deal because the combination would dilute their relative voting power due to Elon's super-voting shares. James notes he has supported this thesis as a "wild card" since roughly two months prior to the video, framing it as a potential poison pill against political opposition to Elon Musk.

3. **Wall Street Skepticism and Coordinated FUD:** Alexandra Merz argues that the current stock price suppression is not natural retail selling, citing a day when the broader market rose ~4% while Tesla did nothing. She accuses institutional investors of using negative analyst notes and coordinated social media activity — including new or dormant X accounts suddenly becoming active critics — to purchase Tesla at lower prices. James echoes this, praising Wall Street for "giving us incredible deals." The panel specifically calls out a JP Morgan analyst note as timed to suppress the stock price rather than provide genuine analysis.
