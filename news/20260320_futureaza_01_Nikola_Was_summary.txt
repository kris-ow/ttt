This video from the "futureaza" channel features a firsthand account from Bob, an engineer and member of the Oregon Electric Vehicle Association, who worked directly on the drivetrain system for the original Nikola Motors semi-truck prototype unveiled in December 2016. Bob reveals previously unseen internal details of the project, arguing that while the truck's electric drivetrain was genuinely impressive engineering, Trevor Milton deliberately misrepresented the vehicle as a hydrogen fuel cell truck and staged a fraudulent rolling demonstration. The video frames Milton's pardon by President Trump as a corrupt outcome for a man who was legitimately convicted of fraud.

## Market & Competition

1. **The Nikola semi-truck's actual drivetrain was a natural gas range-extender electric system, not hydrogen.** Bob's company, EV Drive, was contracted in early 2015 to design and build the electric drive system for the Nikola semi-truck prototype. The system consisted of six PM250 inverters driving six motors across all axles (all-wheel drive), producing approximately 2,000 horsepower. The battery pack was 315 kilowatt-hours using LG NCA 18650 cells configured at 800 volts. The truck also incorporated a natural gas turbine range extender producing approximately 350 horsepower running at 92,000–94,000 RPM, which would have enabled a range of approximately 2,000 miles on combined fuel and electric power. The original plan was a natural gas-powered range-extended electric truck — not a hydrogen fuel cell vehicle.

2. **The December 1, 2016 "World Hydrogen Truck" unveil was staged fraud.** Bob was present at the splashout event and personally unplugged the 120-volt shore power cable that was secretly powering the truck's interior electronics — power that Milton claimed the truck was generating itself. An H2 (hydrogen) sticker had been placed on the front bumper just days before the event, with workers told only that "Trevor said to put it on." Milton announced to an audience that included state governors and trucking industry leaders that this was the world's first hydrogen-powered semi-truck — a claim Bob describes as a "big fat lie." The truck lacked critical gearbox components and was not drivable at the time of the unveil.

3. **The infamous "driving" video released approximately one year later was also fabricated.** After investors pressured Milton to demonstrate the truck actually moving, a video was released showing the truck appearing to drive under its own power at highway speed. Bob's colleague Paul identified that the camera had been tilted to disguise a downhill slope; a reservoir visible in the background appeared at an angle. Paul located the exact spot near Salt Lake City, sent someone out to coast a car downhill in neutral, and confirmed it reached highway speed at that location. The truck rolled downhill by gravity with the camera angled to make it appear level.

4. **Trevor Milton stole intellectual property from Bob's engineering team.** After being given a demonstration of Bob's four-motor electric UTV at the Nikola facility, Milton was enthusiastic about the technology and promised to help commercialize it. Upon returning to Salt Lake City, Milton had his staff quickly file five patents based on Bob's designs — including cut-and-pasted drawings from Bob's own battery module schematics — and put his employees' names on them. Bob states the patent filings contained errors because Milton's team did not fully understand the technology, yet the patents were awarded. This IP theft occurred under NDA, which Bob notes is voided when it would require concealing federal crimes.

5. **Milton's other fabrications included claims of producing hydrogen at $4/kg.** Beyond the truck itself, Milton publicly claimed Nikola was producing hydrogen at $4 per kilogram — a cost that, if real, would have made the company extraordinarily valuable. In reality, Nikola was not producing any hydrogen at all. Bob's colleague Paul became the key whistleblower, working with short-seller Hindenburg Research (who shorted Nikola stock and profited significantly when the fraud was exposed) and with government agencies including the SEC. Milton was eventually indicted, convicted, and sentenced to four years in prison for fraud.

6. **Milton was pardoned by President Trump approximately six months before the video's recording, which Bob characterizes as a corrupt outcome.** Milton had donated approximately $1.8 million to Trump's campaign and hired Brad Bondi (husband of then-Attorney General Pam Bondi) to advocate for his pardon. When asked publicly why he pardoned Milton, Trump cited perceived unfair treatment due to the trial being held in New York while Milton was from Utah. Bob dismisses this justification, stating Milton received a normal legal process and was unambiguously a criminal. Milton's victims lost hundreds of millions of dollars; Milton retains his ill-gotten gains. The Nikola prototype truck was reportedly sold at auction approximately six months prior to the video, with Bob submitting a bid but being outbid.

7. **The electric drivetrain itself was genuinely capable engineering that was delivered on time.** Bob emphasizes that the truck's electric drive system — the portion his team built — was fully assembled, tested, and ready. The truck fell short of being drivable primarily because specialty hardened gears for the two-speed electrically switchable transmissions (engineered by Adrian of AMR Racing) were not completed in time. Had those gears arrived, the 315 kWh battery pack alone would have been sufficient to shake out the truck and demonstrate it driving, independent of the unfinished range extender. EV Drive's contract for the electric drive system alone was $750,000.
