4. **XAI-SpaceX Merger Already Completed:** The video confirms that XAI and SpaceX formally merged in early February (2026). Under the merger, XAI largely operates as its own entity with integration occurring over time. Shotwell supported the merger due to SpaceX's increasing AI usage and the need for deeper AI expertise — SpaceX had already had AI engineers for one to one-and-a-half years prior to the merger. Shotwell now oversees the Colossus supercomputer infrastructure and a 1.2 gigawatt power buildout at XAI's data center, utilizing Tesla's Megapacks. She also now oversees X.

5. **Starlink Scale and Future Satellite Plans:** Starlink has surpassed 10 million customers, with strong growth expected particularly in conflict zones. SpaceX has launched approximately 10,000 Starlink satellites and plans to cap the constellation at 15,000–20,000 satellites, though it holds FCC licensing for well above that number. However, Shotwell confirmed SpaceX has requested FCC licensing for up to 1 million AI satellites to operate at higher orbital shells — separate from Starlink's low Earth orbit constellation — which would conduct AI inference and communicate to Earth via the Starlink network. Larry notes that the upcoming Starlink Version 3 satellites each have the capacity equivalent to roughly 30 or more current-generation satellites.

6. **SpaceX Launch Activity:** SpaceX completed 165 Falcon 9 launches in 2025, representing over 85% of all US orbital flights. The expectation for 2026 is 140–145 Falcon 9 launches, tapering as Starship ramps up. SpaceX has also built over 600 total successful Falcon 9 launches, over 500 Falcon 9 second stages, and over 100 Falcon 9 boosters. The Starship program currently has 18 vehicles in various stages of construction at its Texas factory, which encompasses 1 million square feet of floor space built within the last two years.

7. **Moon and Mars Strategy:** SpaceX's current focus is on lunar landing and settlement, with Shotwell stating the human landing system should be ready by 2028 and humans on the lunar surface before 2030. The longer-term goal is a manufacturing facility on the moon within 10 years, ideally five. Shotwell stressed Mars has not been abandoned — the moon is receiving more near-term energy due to plans for data centers in space, mass drivers, and producing AI satellites on the moon using lunar materials. The plan calls for robots to build lunar infrastructure first, followed by human settlement, with a million people needed for a self-sustaining off-world civilization. NASA's Artemis program has SpaceX contracted for Artemis 3 and Blue Origin contracted for Artemis 5.
