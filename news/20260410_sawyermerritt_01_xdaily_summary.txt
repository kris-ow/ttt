Merritt notes this equates to approximately 1,000 miles every 4.5 seconds. This is a meaningful signal for both the autonomous driving thesis (wider adoption, more training data accumulation) and as a proxy for FSD attach rate growth. Higher daily miles also feed directly into Tesla's safety-per-mile narrative and improve the real-world dataset advantage over competitors.

2. **FSD Europe — Potential Catalyst Tomorrow (April 11):** Merritt posted cryptically that April 11, 2026 is "potentially a massive day for FSD in Europe," without elaborating further. This is a high-interest teaser with no additional specifics provided. Given the regulatory complexity of FSD approval in European markets, any approval or supervised FSD launch in Europe would be a significant catalyst for Tesla's international autonomous driving expansion. Investors should watch for announcements on April 11.

3. **Robotaxi App Update — Operational Refinements:** Tesla updated its Robotaxi app with several notable changes: a new **tips section** showing available seat count, informing riders that vehicle lights will pulse upon arrival, and a disclosure that Tesla may use the **interior cabin camera for safety monitoring**. The app also received a darker visual appearance. These changes suggest Tesla is actively iterating on the rider experience and safety disclosure framework ahead of broader deployment — consistent with a service approaching more public-facing scale.

4. **Model Y L International Expansion — Mixed Demand Signals:** Tesla officially launched the larger **Model Y L in Israel**, with first deliveries beginning in June. However, in Australia, estimated delivery dates for new Model Y L orders have **pushed out to July–August 2026**, slipping from the prior May–June window — possibly indicating production constraints or logistics prioritization. Meanwhile, Tesla raised the price of the Model Y L in **South Korea by 7.7%**, which could reflect FX adjustments, demand strength, or tariff pass-throughs. Tesla also discontinued the white interior on all Model Y Premium trims built at **Giga Shanghai**, replacing it with the new "Zen Grey" interior across markets including Australia, New Zealand, South Korea, Japan, and the Philippines.

5. **Market & Competition — Legacy Automakers Alarmed by China:** A high-engagement post (6.3K likes, 1.2K reposts) highlighted stark statements from Toyota and Honda CEOs about Chinese EV competition. Toyota's CEO warned "unless things change, we will not survive," and Honda's CEO stated "we have no chance against this" after visiting China. While not directly about Tesla, this underscores the competitive pressure reshaping the global auto market — a dynamic that could benefit Tesla's positioning as a technology-first EV/AV company relative to legacy OEMs. Separately, Tesla reportedly added **China-based Sunwoda** to its global battery supply chain, introducing LFP battery technology focused on faster charging — a supply chain diversification move.

6. **Waymo/Waze Pothole Partnership — Competitor Activity:** Waymo announced a partnership with Waze to use autonomous fleet data to help cities detect and repair potholes, launching pilots in San Francisco Bay Area, Los Angeles, Phoenix, Austin, and Atlanta. This is a competitor development worth monitoring — Waymo is actively expanding its geographic footprint across five major U.S. cities while building municipal relationships. For Tesla's robotaxi thesis, Waymo's multi-city presence reinforces the competitive race for urban AV market share.
