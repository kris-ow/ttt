## Key Posts / Themes

1. **FSD V14.3.1 Rolls Out — Wide Release Candidate:** FSD V14.3.1 began rolling out on April 14, generating significant engagement (2.6K likes on the announcement post). Sawyer Merritt drove the new version on his Model Y and called it "polished vs 14.3" and in his opinion "ready for wide release." Notable behavior changes include a new parking menu accessible via a "P" icon with multiple parking options, and Mad Max highway mode described as slightly more conservative — the vehicle never exceeded 85 mph even with clear road ahead, and moved to the right lane for off-ramps far earlier than previous versions. These behavioral refinements suggest Tesla is tuning for safety profile consistency ahead of broader deployment or regulatory review.

2. **Cybercab Testing Expands to Florida:** Two Cybercabs were spotted testing in Naples, Florida, marking the first confirmed Cybercab testing activity in the state. The post drew 3K likes and 203 reposts, making it one of the day's highest-engagement items. This geographic expansion of Cybercab testing is a direct catalyst signal, indicating Tesla is broadening its pre-deployment validation footprint beyond previously known testing regions.

3. **FSD European Regulatory Pathway Advances:** Following the Netherlands' prior approval of Tesla FSD Supervised, Norway's road authority (SVV) issued a clarifying statement explaining that the approval now enters a broader European review process, where regulators across the EU will evaluate the decision, ask questions, and ultimately work toward a unified determination. This is a meaningful step in the EU regulatory pipeline, though the timeline to full approval remains unspecified.

4. **Tesla Safety Score v3.0 — FSD Scoring Overhaul:** Tesla released Safety Score v3.0, which now awards a score of 100 for every mile driven on FSD (Supervised). Previously, FSD usage could negatively affect Safety Score, which was a major user complaint. This change removes a disincentive to use FSD, potentially increasing supervised FSD engagement and accelerating the accumulation of training miles.

5. **Giga Shanghai Positioned for Humanoid Robot Manufacturing:** A Tesla China executive stated in an interview that "like other Tesla factories, Giga Shanghai can shoulder important responsibilities in manufacturing all-new products, including robots," adding they are "highly confident in welcoming the arrival of a new era of robots." While no specific production numbers or timelines were given, this is a direct on-record statement tying Giga Shanghai's capacity to Optimus manufacturing.

6. **$325M Megapack Expansion Underway in Australia:** Work has begun on a $325 million third-stage expansion of the Western Downs Battery in Queensland, Australia, described as one of the largest batteries in the world and expected to nearly double the existing installation. This is a concrete, named project with a dollar figure, representing continued Megapack deployment momentum.

7. **Australian Used EV Market — Tesla Model 3 and Y Lead:** In March 2026, the Tesla Model 3 and Model Y were the top two bestselling used EVs in Australia, with total used EV sales rising 138% from February. 7,557 used EVs were sold in March. While this is a secondary market metric, the scale of the monthly jump and Tesla's continued dominance signals sustained brand strength in a cost-sensitive environment.

8. **Competitive Landscape — Mercedes 2027 EQS and Nissan Juke EV:** Mercedes unveiled the redesigned all-electric 2027 EQS with approximately 400 miles of expected EPA range, a 122 kWh battery using silicon oxide + graphite chemistry, and optional steer-by-wire — the first German production car with that feature. Nissan separately unveiled the all-electric Juke for Europe, with pricing and specs to be announced later and a 2027 on-sale date. Both signal continued incumbent EV portfolio expansion, though neither directly threatens Tesla's FSD/autonomy differentiation thesis.
