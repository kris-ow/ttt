This video from Jo Bhakdi offers a pre-weekend macro market update focused on the geopolitical situation between the US, Israel, and Iran, and how it could impact Tesla's stock price in the near term. The core argument is that the market has not yet fully priced in a long-term military conflict, and that an escalation — which Jo assigns a 75% probability — could trigger a rapid 10–20% drop in the broader market, pulling Tesla down with it regardless of its underlying business fundamentals.

## Market & Competition
1. **Macro risk overriding Tesla fundamentals:** Jo argues that AI and tech stocks, including Tesla and Nvidia, are being "hammered" by macro forces, and that in crisis situations Tesla will simply follow the broader market's beta. He explicitly states that discussions about Optimus, robotaxi, or any Tesla-specific catalyst are irrelevant to short-term price action during a geopolitical shock, as Tesla will move with the market.

2. **Iran escalation probability and market impact:** Jo assigns a 75% or greater probability of military escalation — including boots on the ground and an attempt to take what he refers to as "Karg Island" — occurring over the weekend or by the following week, with only a 25% chance of a peace resolution. He argues the market has still not priced in a long-term conflict, noting the NASDAQ is only down approximately 6.68% year-to-date from where the Iran situation began heating up.

3. **Expected market drop scenario:** Jo estimates that if a long-term conflict materializes, the market (NASDAQ) will drop a total of 10–20% from the start of the Iran escalation, implying an additional 7–8% drop from current levels. He describes this repricing as likely to be rapid rather than gradual, drawing on the historical pattern of markets dropping sharply when war begins and then grinding higher as the conflict becomes "priced in."

4. **Oil futures signal current mispricing:** Jo points to crude oil futures as evidence the market is currently pricing in de-escalation — with futures showing approximately $94/barrel currently, declining to $93 in May, $90 in June, and $87 in July. He argues this de-escalatory curve is likely mispriced given the probability of conflict, and that an escalation would produce a shock-wave inversion in the futures curve, with oil potentially spiking to $100–$120 before declining, rippling through the broader global economy.

5. **Tesla price target in a drop scenario:** Jo calculates that if the NASDAQ drops ~8% from current levels and Tesla follows its own beta (approximately double the index move, or ~16%), Tesla — which he cites at approximately $380 — could fall to around $330–$340. He notes this would be a purely macro-driven move unrelated to Tesla's business. He considers this a potential buying opportunity, particularly if Cybercab and FSD catalysts materialize in Q2 2026.
