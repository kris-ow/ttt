This video from the "brighterwithherbert" channel provides a comprehensive global status report on Tesla's Full Self-Driving rollout, arguing that FSD has crossed a critical inflection point from promise to deployed product. The host contends that the mainstream perception of FSD is dramatically lagging behind the reality — FSD Supervised is live in seven countries, unsupervised operation is active in geofenced areas of Austin, and a wave of regulatory approvals across Europe and Asia appears imminent. The core thesis is that the market is still pricing FSD as a vehicle feature rather than a global software platform, and that the gap between perception and reality represents a major investment opportunity.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Supervised Now Live in Seven Countries:** FSD Supervised is currently approved and available to customers in the United States (live since 2023), Canada, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea (launched November 2025). South Korea has already logged 5 million FSD miles in just 100 days, with the Korea Expressway Corporation rating highway performance as "excellent." China has partial approval in limited form, but full deployment is constrained by data localization laws — Tesla cannot transfer training videos outside China, and the government banned the term "Full Self-Driving," requiring Tesla to rename it "Intelligent Assisted Driving." Elon Musk has said he expects full approval in China in 2026.

2. **Unsupervised FSD Active in Geofenced Austin:** Fully unsupervised FSD — requiring no driver — is currently active within geofenced areas of Austin, Texas. This is explicitly noted as limited to one city and small geofenced boundaries, not a broad nationwide deployment. The host flags that any expansion of the unsupervised geofenced zone to new cities will be a key signal that nationwide rollout is accelerating.

3. **Netherlands Expected to Be First EU Approval on April 10, 2026:** The Dutch Vehicle Authority (RDW) has been conducting an 18-month testing program with Tesla. Tesla completed 1.66 million km of testing on European roads and conducted over 13,000 customer ride-alongs. All documentation for UN Regulation 171 approval and Article 39 exemptions has been submitted. The RDW confirmed in late March 2026 that it is in the final internal review stage, with an expected decision date of April 10, 2026. The host explicitly notes this is an expected date, not a confirmed approval, and that regulatory decisions can be delayed or come with conditions.

4. **EU Mutual Recognition Could Unlock 27 Countries from One Approval:** Under EU Regulation 2018/858, once the Netherlands grants type approval, other EU member states can recognize that approval through a mutual recognition process, potentially allowing one approval to open the door to all 27 EU countries without each running its own full review. Tesla anticipates a coordinated EU-wide rollout by summer 2026, covering Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, Austria, and the Nordic countries, among others. Tesla has been running FSD demonstration ride-alongs across Germany, France, Italy, Denmark, and Switzerland through March 31, 2026, extending the program multiple times due to popularity.

5. **Additional Pending Markets — Israel, Japan, Abu Dhabi:** Israel's Transport Minister publicly stated FSD is "coming soon," following approval of supervised autonomous driving trials on public roads in February 2026. Japan's Tesla President Hiroshi Hashimoto confirmed FSD is targeting a launch in Japan in 2026, with testing actively underway in Tokyo using both Model Y and Model 3 vehicles — an over-the-air update could instantly activate FSD for approximately 40,000 Teslas already on Japanese roads. Abu Dhabi launched supervised FSD trials on public roads on March 1, 2026, under the Abu Dhabi Mobility Authority — the first confirmed FSD footprint in the Middle East — though this is a structured trial, not yet a full consumer launch.

6. **FSD Version 14.3 — "Last Big Piece of the Puzzle" — Imminent:** Elon Musk confirmed on March 19, 2026, that FSD version 14.3 is currently in testing, with a wide release expected in early to mid-April 2026. Musk described version 14.3 as where "the last big piece of the puzzle lands," citing an order-of-magnitude larger neural network, more reasoning capabilities, and reinforcement learning. Musk has described the expected outcome as making the car feel "almost like a sentient being." This version is distinct from prior iterations in that FSD version 14 unified perception, prediction, and planning into a single end-to-end neural network rather than separate modules.

7. **Pedestrian Intent Prediction Clip — Technical Significance:** A widely circulated clip this week showed Tesla's FSD system slowing and yielding for a pedestrian who was still standing on the sidewalk — before any movement toward the road — and the pedestrian then stepped off the curb seconds later, exactly as the AI anticipated. The host explains this demonstrates prediction of human intent rather than reactive detection, made possible by the system reading body language cues such as body orientation, head direction, and weight shift. A human driver in the same clip did not yield. The host argues this capability — giving 1–2 extra seconds of reaction time — is the type of safety demonstration regulators specifically want to see, as pedestrian accidents are the single most common type of fatal accident.

8. **Safety Data — Approximately Seven Times Safer Than Human Drivers:** Tesla's Q2 2025 safety report showed FSD Supervised vehicles were involved in approximately one crash every 7.6 million miles driven, compared to a national average of roughly one crash per 670,000 miles for human drivers — approximately seven times safer on a per-mile basis. Elon Musk posted this week that Tesla AI self-driving "will be greater than 10 times safer than human driving," which the host clarifies is a forward-looking projection, not a current confirmed statistic. The host acknowledges methodological limitations: Tesla's numbers are self-reported, not independently verified, and FSD may operate more frequently in favorable conditions.

9. **Tesla's Cumulative Training Data Advantage:** Tesla has accumulated 8.44 billion miles of real-world driving data — more than all other autonomous driving programs combined, according to the host. The host argues this data advantage compounds over time, as more deployment generates more edge cases, more training data, and continued safety improvements.
