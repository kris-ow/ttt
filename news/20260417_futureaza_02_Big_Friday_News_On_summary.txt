This weekly podcast episode from the futureaza channel covers a broad range of Tesla news from the week of April 17, 2026, including a new software spring update, AI chip developments, Giga Berlin manufacturing insights, the end of the Model S and X lineup, Cybercab testing updates, FSD approval in the EU, and U.S. EV sales data. The hosts — joined by Matthew, Joe, and Brian — discuss Tesla's competitive advantages across software, manufacturing, and autonomous driving while contextualizing the week's stock recovery from recent lows.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Tesla Spring Software Update and FSD 14.3.1 Wide Release:** Tesla dropped a notable spring software update, which the hosts say is less common than the typical holiday update cycle. Key highlights include one-tap FSD subscription with live stats, Grok voice AI with location reminders, Pet Mode expanded to include dog, cat, hedgehog, and custom pet names, higher-quality visualization for Model 3 and Model Y, blind spot accent lights using interior side lights, turn signals and park safety improvements, immersive sound stage on premium audio, a new dash cam for Model 3 and Model Y supporting up to 24 hours of local permanent storage (up from roughly one hour while driving), and automatic overnight updates with improved weather maps. The hosts also note that FSD version 14.3.1 has been pushed to wide release, though not all owners may have received it yet.

2. **Tesla Service Updated with Grok AI Integration:** Tesla has integrated Grok AI into its service workflow, allowing owners to upload photos of issues, describe problems, and receive troubleshooting steps — with prompts to schedule a service appointment if needed. One host noted that when he went to request service for an interior camera shroud cleaning issue, the car had already identified what he was coming in for and pre-populated the appointment reason. The hosts frame this as a meaningful improvement to what they acknowledge has historically been an Achilles heel for Tesla.

3. **Model S and Model X Signature Editions Sold Out — End of an Era:** The final Signature editions of the Model S and Model X have both sold out. Matthew noted that for the first time in over 15 years, the Tesla showroom at Bellevue Square Mall has no Model S or Model X on display. He shared that the showroom also struggled to keep Cybertruck units in stock. The hosts speculate that the Model S will not receive a replacement, with the Roadster expected to serve as Tesla's top performance vehicle, and the Model X being succeeded by the Model Y L and a Cybertruck-based platform. Buyers of the Signature vehicles are subject to a one-year resale restriction, similar to the Foundation Series. Matthew advised owners to drive them rather than treat them as investments, noting that even original Roadsters are selling at or below original MSRP.

4. **Cybertruck Leads EV Truck Sales in Q1 2026:** According to Cox Automotive data cited in the episode, Cybertruck sold 3,519 units in Q1 2026, leading all electric trucks. Ford came in second with approximately 2,000 units, and Rivian third with approximately 1,600. In the broader EV market, Model Y led all EVs with 78,000 units, followed by Model 3 at 31,000. The next closest competitor was the BZ4X at 10,000 units, followed by Honda at 9,000. The Rivian R1S moved only approximately 5,000 units. The hosts noted the F-150 Lightning has effectively exited the market. The hosts believe the gap between Tesla and other EV brands will maintain or widen, particularly as tax credits are removed, and noted that the used Tesla market is currently very favorable for buyers with prices as low as $20,000–$25,000.

5. **Giga Berlin Manufacturing Video — 4680 Cells and Robotic Assembly:** Tesla Manufacturing posted a video featuring Lars touring Giga Berlin. Key observations from the hosts include confirmation that 4680 battery cells are now being used at Giga Berlin across multiple Model Y versions, and that robots are being used to attach front fenders and front hoods on the body-in-white line. Additionally, a manufacturing technique developed at Giga Berlin — related to trimming giga castings — is being rolled out to other Tesla factories including Giga Shanghai and either Fremont or Giga Texas. The hosts describe this as a powerful example of Tesla's "machine that builds the machine" philosophy, where cross-department employees contribute unexpected innovations.

6. **Model Y Tent at Fremont Still in Use, Cybercab Spotted on Test Track:** The host described a recent visit to the Fremont factory area, noting that the original Model 3 production tent has been converted to a Model Y assembly tent and remains active. The host also captured video of a Model 3 and Model Y driving themselves from a Supercharger to the shipping yard. Multiple Cybercabs were observed testing on the legacy test track behind the factory — infrastructure originally built during the GM/NUMMI/Toyota era. A separate test track is currently being built in Texas.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Supervised Approved in the Netherlands, EU Rollout Beginning:** FSD Supervised has received regulatory approval in the Netherlands and will begin rolling out shortly. The hosts noted it appears to apply to new vehicle sales initially, with Tesla potentially needing to apply separately in each EU member state for pre-owned and already-delivered vehicles. Matthew indicated that within a couple of months, anyone buying a new Tesla in the Netherlands should be able to use FSD right away. Belgium also appears to be progressing toward approval. The hosts expect a domino effect across EU member states and anticipate a new wave of European FSD videos.

2. **Tesla Insurance Safety Score V3 — FSD Miles Score 100:** Tesla's latest Safety Score V3 update gives every mile driven with FSD Supervised a perfect score of 100, which over time results in a higher average safety score and lower monthly insurance premiums. This update applies to new policies in Indiana, Tennessee, Texas, Arizona, Virginia, and Illinois. The hosts shared mixed personal experiences with Tesla Insurance — one host uses it on his Model Y Juniper but switched away from it for his Cybertruck after premiums doubled. Matthew noted Tesla Insurance is not yet available in Washington state and flagged concerns about difficulty reaching claims support when needed.

## Robotaxi

1. **Austin Unsupervised Robotaxi Fleet Grows to 12 Vehicles:** Tesla has added another unsupervised Model Y robotaxi to its Austin fleet, bringing the total to 12 vehicles. The host also noted that Tesla has converted the south employee parking lot at its Bay Area headquarters into a robotaxi fleet yard, with approximately 100 vehicles observed in flyover footage. The host indicated Tesla has multiple staging locations across the 400-square-mile Austin operating area for cleaning, prepping, and deploying vehicles.

2. **Cybercab Spotted in Wichita, Kansas — Broad Geographic Testing Underway:** A Cybercab was spotted and filmed in Wichita, Kansas, adding to sightings across multiple states including Oklahoma and New York. The hosts referenced Joe Tegtmeyer's recent video showing nearly 100 Cybercabs being used as test-bed vehicles for different regions. The hosts noted Tesla is no longer hiding these vehicles and is conducting broad geographic testing in varied weather and road conditions.

## Humanoid Bots

1. **AI5 Chip Confirmed for Optimus and Supercomputer Clusters, Not Cars:** Elon Musk confirmed via a reply on X that the AI5 chip is destined for Optimus robots and Tesla's supercomputer clusters — not for vehicles. Elon stated that AI4 is sufficient to achieve significantly better-than-human safety for FSD. The hosts advised against waiting to purchase a Tesla in anticipation of AI5 being added to cars, comparing it to waiting for the best Pentium chip in the 1990s. One host speculated AI5 might eventually reach vehicles around 2028, though this was framed as speculation rather than confirmed information.

2. **AI5 Taped Out 45 Days Ahead of Schedule; AI6 Details Shared:** A Tesla chip engineer shared on X that the AI5 chip was completed 45 days ahead of schedule, though some design concessions were made to achieve that timeline. AI6 is described as addressing those concessions and delivering a "true doubling of performance" over AI5 in the same package size using Samsung's 2NB fab in Texas, with further performance improvements on TSMC's 2nm node in Arizona. AI6 also dedicates half of its computation accelerator to SRAM, providing memory bandwidth described as "an order of magnitude greater" than DRAM bandwidth for SRAM-cached calculations. Elon also mentioned AI6 and Dojo 3 as additional chips in the works.

## Market & Competition

1. **Tesla Stock Recovered Above $400 After Near-20% Drop:** The hosts noted Tesla stock had dropped close to 20% (potentially hitting $340) before recovering to above $400 during the week of the recording. The hosts praised those who bought the dip, particularly at $350 or $340.

2. **BYD Factory Fire in China:** A major fire broke out at a BYD factory in China during construction of exterior insulation. There were no reported casualties. The hosts noted the fire consumed a significant portion of the structure and described it as a notable industry event.

3. **Starlink Mobile Service Launch with US Mobile:** US Mobile and Starlink launched a mobile service in the United States. The hosts discussed Starlink's particular strength in remote areas where traditional carriers like T-Mobile have coverage gaps, while noting questions remain about coverage in buildings and dense urban areas.

4. **Norway Autonomous Bus Pilot — First Driverless Passenger Bus:** Norway launched its first pilot of a bus carrying passengers in regular traffic without a human safety driver, described as a historic first in Norwegian history.
