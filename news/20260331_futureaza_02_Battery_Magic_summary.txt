This Monday news roundup from March 30, 2026 covers a broad range of topics including the surge in US grid battery manufacturing, the global rise of solar energy, EV demand spikes driven by high oil prices, Tesla's Q1 2026 delivery consensus, Waymo/robotaxi developments, and a handful of Tesla product and infrastructure updates. The host's overarching thesis is that energy transition tailwinds — from cheap solar to expensive gasoline — are accelerating EV adoption globally, while Tesla continues to quietly improve its infrastructure and operational efficiency.

## Energy

1. **US Grid Battery Manufacturing Surge:** The US has made surprising strides in domestic battery manufacturing for grid-scale energy storage, reducing dependence on Chinese imports — though the host notes that graphite, electrodes, and anodes are still largely imported, with domestication efforts underway. The key driver was a "big battery building credit" that attracted manufacturers, who pivoted toward grid storage rather than EVs as the primary application.

2. **Sodium-Ion and Emerging Battery Technologies:** Sodium-ion batteries are described as increasingly capable, with competitive gravimetric energy density, though volumetric density (physical bulk) remains a limitation. The host notes this makes them better suited for stationary grid storage than vehicles. A vague reference is also made to "quantum batteries," which the host treats skeptically.

3. **Solid-State Battery Claim from MG:** MG, the Chinese-owned brand with British heritage, has announced mass production solid-state batteries — notable because it is neither BMW nor Toyota, the companies most associated with repeated unfulfilled solid-state announcements. The host is cautiously skeptical, noting this technology claim remains "in the works."

4. **Battery Imbalance Problem in Grid Storage:** An unnamed 350 megawatt-hour battery storage installation is reportedly delivering significantly less than expected capacity — approximately 75% — due to cell imbalance that the battery management system failed to detect. The host notes this creates chronic overestimation of tradable energy volumes and potentially five-figure weekly balancing costs, but criticizes the article for withholding the identity of the site, the battery maker, and the operator.

5. **Solar Winning the Energy Race:** Solar is described as the world's cheapest power source and is scaling rapidly. China installed more solar in December alone than the entire US did for the full year. The US currently ranks third globally in solar expansion, with 267 gigawatts of installed capacity supplying approximately 8% of total US electricity demand, up from just 1% in 2015. South Korea is mandating solar panels over public parking lots, a model the host endorses.

6. **Megapack at Charging Stations:** The host notes that Electrify America has Tesla Megapacks deployed at many of its charging locations to buffer grid demand — analogous to how gas stations store fuel in underground tanks rather than drawing directly from tankers.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Australian EV Sales Explosion:** EV demand in Australia has surged dramatically following a spike in petrol prices linked to US military conflict with Iran. Petrol reached approximately AU$2.54 per liter (roughly US$9.61 per gallon equivalent). The result has been a 76% spike in EV searches and a 95% year-on-year jump in EV sales. Australian car dealers are reportedly turning away non-buying customers due to overwhelming demand.

2. **Global Oil Disruption Driving EV Demand:** Approximately 20% of the world's oil flows through a strait currently controlled by Iran. The host references a Clean Technica article arguing this geopolitical dynamic is driving up EV demand across multiple markets, not just Australia.

3. **Tesla Q1 2026 Delivery Consensus:** The Wall Street delivery consensus for Tesla's Q1 2026 is approximately 365,000 units, compiled from analysts including Deutsche Bank, Canaccord, and others. The host describes this figure as "kind of low" and notes he will be publishing his own estimate on Wednesday (April 1).

4. **High Gas Prices Unlikely to Boost Q1 Deliveries:** Despite high oil prices, the host argues Tesla will not see a meaningful delivery bump this quarter because Tesla runs lean inventory with low days of supply, and the oil price spike happened too late in the quarter to drive meaningful additional sales volume.

5. **Model 3 Production at Giga Berlin:** The host advocates for Tesla to begin building the Model 3 at Giga Berlin, arguing it would reduce the European price of the Model 3 by eliminating the approximately 14–15% import tariff, and would open export opportunities to additional markets.

6. **Model 3 in Canada — Tariff Complications:** The host notes that Model 3 units imported to Canada from the US face tariffs, while Chinese-built Model 3s face separate barriers. To qualify for preferential import treatment in Canada, vehicles must be priced under CA$35,000 (approximately US$28,000), a threshold the China-built Model 3 does not meet.

7. **Lucid's Record Deliveries but Flat Gross Profit:** Lucid is on track to extend its streak of eight consecutive quarters of record deliveries but has failed to improve gross profitability. Rivian, by contrast, has made notable progress toward gross profitability. Lucid remains dependent on the Saudi sovereign wealth fund, which has pledged an additional $4 billion — an investment that could dilute current Lucid shareholders by 65–70% depending on the strike price at which those funds are deployed.

8. **Tesla New Folding Supercharger Units:** Tesla's new pre-fabricated folding supercharger units (referred to as "FUS") cut installation time in half, save approximately 20% on cost, and improve build quality. The majority of cost savings come from civil, electrical, and logistics expenses.

## Robotaxi

1. **Tesla Lowers Minimum Robotaxi Rider Age:** Tesla has lowered the minimum age for riding in its robotaxi from 13 to 8 years old. An adult or guardian is still required to accompany the child. The host also notes that on X (formerly Twitter), Tesla vehicles are increasingly being spotted operating with no passengers — fully driverless.

2. **Robotaxi Infrastructure Questions:** A viewer question raises the issue of cleaning, charging, and maintenance infrastructure for robotaxi fleets not yet being fully deployed. The host speculates that service centers could double as overnight cleaning and maintenance hubs, and expresses hope that fleet managers can get early access to the service as they would represent a significant early success story.

## Humanoid Bots

1. **First Markets for Optimus Disruption:** When asked which markets Optimus will disrupt first, the host identifies factories, logistics, and warehouses — all behind-the-scenes industrial settings — as the most likely early deployment sectors.

## Market & Competition

1. **Chinese Solar Panel Tariffs and US Competitiveness:** Chinese solar panels face a 100% import tariff in the US. The host argues that US-made panels only need to be priced within approximately 40–50% of Chinese production costs to remain competitive after tariffs, and notes proximity to US deployment sites as an additional logistics advantage.

2. **GLJ Research Criticized:** The host criticizes GLJ Research's Gordon Johnson harshly, arguing his record on Tesla calls has been "impressively bad" and questioning the undisclosed funding behind his bearish research. The host suggests the SEC should investigate.
