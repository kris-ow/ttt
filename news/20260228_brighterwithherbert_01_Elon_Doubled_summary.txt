6. The "all-in" debate: No financial advisor would formally recommend full concentration in one stock, but asset concentration is historically how outsized wealth is created. The key distinction is that passive investors cannot control company direction the way founders like Musk can.

7. Stock behavior pattern: Research shows nearly all of Tesla's historical price appreciation occurred in approximately 42 key trading days. Most gains happen outside market hours. This makes market timing extremely difficult and favors long-term holding.

Asymmetric robo taxi catalysts to watch: Removing safety drivers from vehicles, Cybercab production scale-up, national autonomous vehicle legislation, FSD unsupervised rollout to AI4 hardware, and international regulatory approvals each represent discrete events that could cause sudden large stock moves.

10. Investing philosophy takeaway: Stock market returns are driven by a tiny number of "lottery winner" companies (research by Hendrik Bessembinder at Arizona State University). The average stock over its lifetime generates a negative return. If you identify a winner, selling too early is a major risk.

Key Conclusions: Elon's repeated public bullishness and his $1 billion personal purchase are unusual signals of CEO confidence. Tesla's near-term story is robo taxi; the longer-term, larger story is Optimus. Investors should match their strategy to their time horizon and temperament — short-term traders face an unpredictable stock, while long-term holders are betting on fundamental business transformation that Wall Street has not yet fully priced in.
