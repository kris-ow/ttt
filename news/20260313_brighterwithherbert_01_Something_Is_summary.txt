This video covers Tesla's February 2026 sales performance in China, the state of FSD development and competition in the Chinese autonomous driving market, the Model Y Long spotted in Australia and South Korea ahead of expected launches, and Audi's CEO declaring gas engines have a future well into the 2030s. The core argument is that while Tesla's China numbers are solid in context (best February ever, Model Y outselling the Xiaomi YU7), the real demand catalyst will be FSD approval in China — and despite competitive claims from Chinese automakers, Tesla's autonomous driving lead remains substantial globally. Guest Brian White of the Futurza YouTube channel provides firsthand observations from three recent trips to China, including a direct test of Xpeng's XIMO VA 2.0 system.

1. **Tesla China February Sales — Best February Ever, But Context Matters:** Giga Shanghai wholesale numbers came in at 58,599 vehicles (Model 3 and Model Y combined) in February 2026, a 91% year-over-year surge. However, Brian White cautions against a "victory lap" — the comparison is flattering primarily because Giga Shanghai was shut down for Model Y retooling in February 2025. Month-over-month, sales were actually down 15%. Within China specifically, Tesla sold approximately 38,000 vehicles domestically, with the Model Y accounting for 66% and Model 3 the remaining 34%. The Model Y was the second best-selling vehicle in China for February by domestic sales. Importantly, BYD itself reported a 30%+ decline in its own market over the same period, making Tesla's performance relatively strong by industry comparison.

2. **Model Y Outsells Xiaomi YU7 in February:** The Xiaomi YU7 — widely hyped as a "Model Y killer" — was outsold by the Model Y in February. Brian White argues the "killer" framing is overblown: China's market is large enough for multiple strong sellers, and Tesla is effectively capacity-constrained at Giga Shanghai without a new factory. He notes Xiaomi has genuine advantages as a domestic brand with deep tech engineering, strong consumer brand recognition, and a manufacturing partnership that helped it avoid "production hell." However, its competition with Tesla is currently limited to China.

3. **Top-Selling Vehicle in China by Wholesale:** On a wholesale basis (including exports from Giga Shanghai), the Model Y was the top-selling vehicle in China in February with 41,440 units. The number one domestic sales vehicle was the Geely Xingyuan, priced at approximately 70,000 RMB (~$10,000) — a completely different market segment. Brian White notes that Tesla's margin on a single Model Y likely exceeds the entire retail cost of the Geely Xingyuan, making direct unit comparisons misleading.

4. **FSD in China — Competitive Dynamics and Brian White's Firsthand Assessment:** Xpeng CEO He Xiaopeng publicly stated that Tesla's unsupervised Full Self-Driving has "not yet been realized" and declared China the global leader in autonomous driving, predicting L4 autonomy will be fully deployed within designated areas in China within 1–3 years. Brian White, who tested Xpeng's XIMO VA 2.0 system twice within six weeks, offers a more nuanced view: the system improved noticeably between visits — less aggressive at stoplights, smoother starts and stops, good merges — and handled complex Chinese traffic scenarios including 30 scooters driving against traffic on a one-way street. His overall assessment: Xpeng's VA 2.0 is comparable to "a very early version 13" of Tesla FSD. He also notes that FSD in China is not as capable as it is in the US due to less localized training data. Xpeng has received a permit for robotaxi testing and is running an internal pilot in Guangzhou, with hopes for a public beta by end of 2026.

5. **FSD vs. Robotaxi — A Key Technical Distinction:** Brian White relays an insight from Xpeng's lead product manager for VA 2.0: robotaxi is actually the *easier* problem compared to full consumer FSD deployment. Robotaxi operates with remote oversight and controlled geofenced areas, while FSD must work reliably at any hour, in any scenario, without warning — such as a mass evacuation during a typhoon at 3 a.m. This framing supports Tesla's broader thesis that its approach to unsupervised FSD is the harder and more defensible technical achievement.

6. **Model Y Long Spotted in Australia and South Korea:** The 6-seat Model Y Long (YL) variant has been photographed undisguised in Australia and camouflaged in South Korea, signaling imminent availability in both markets. Brian White notes both countries are served by Giga Shanghai, which is already producing the vehicle, making rapid market expansion logistically straightforward. He also mentions the Model Y L has been spotted testing at the Nürburgring in Germany and on drone footage at Giga Berlin, and claims to have insight from sources in Texas suggesting the Model Y L may also be produced in the US — potentially at Giga Texas — though he allows it could alternatively be a "Cyber SUV."

7. **Audi CEO Doubles Down on Gas Engines:** Audi's CEO told Car and Driver that "faltering EV sales and strong interest in gas" are leading Audi to maintain flexibility with internal combustion engine powertrains, stating that "drivetrain concepts will definitely swing back to ICE." He suggested battery electric may not come back fast enough and that Audi may need to develop a next-generation combustion platform if US demand for gas cars persists into the 2030s. Brian White dismisses this as a failure of vision, drawing a parallel to how Tesla created EV demand with the Model 3 rather than waiting for it — and warning that by 2030, autonomous capability will be a standard consumer expectation, leaving ICE-focused brands playing "the wrong game entirely."
