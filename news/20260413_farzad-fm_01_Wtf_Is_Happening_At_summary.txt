## Market & Competition

1. **SpaceX's reported $2 trillion IPO filing:** SpaceX has reportedly confidentially filed to go public at a $2 trillion valuation, which would make it the largest IPO in human history, surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 offering that raised $29 billion. SpaceX is reportedly looking to raise between $50 and $75 billion in a single offering. Morgan Stanley is the front runner to lead the offering, with 21 banks reportedly involved in the deal. Morgan Stanley's E*Trade platform is reportedly being set up for retail allocation of up to 30%, which the host describes as unprecedented, potentially allowing ordinary brokerage account holders to buy in for the first time.

2. **SpaceX's current market dominance:** In 2025, SpaceX launched 165 orbital missions on Falcon 9 alone — roughly one launch every two days — and now controls approximately 82% of the global commercial launch market. The host draws an analogy to one airline controlling 82% of all flights on Earth. Competitors include ULA's Vulcan rocket (priced around $4,000 per kilogram to orbit), Europe's Ariane 6 ($4,000–$6,000 per kilogram), and Boeing's Starliner crew capsule, which has been plagued with helium leaks, software issues, and delays costing Boeing over $1 billion in overruns.

3. **SpaceX as a telecom company, not just a rocket company:** The host argues that SpaceX is now primarily a telecommunications company that also builds rockets. Starlink, its satellite internet constellation, has over 10,000 satellites in orbit and more than 9 million subscribers worldwide. Starlink alone generated an estimated $10–12 billion in revenue in 2025. Total company revenue was approximately $15–16 billion in 2025, with roughly $8 billion in profit. Elon Musk is quoted as saying NASA will account for only about 5% of SpaceX revenue in 2026, with the vast majority coming from commercial Starlink. SpaceX also holds over $15 billion in government contract backlog through 2030.

4. **Valuation debate and analyst projections:** Critics note that $2 trillion on $15 billion in revenue implies a price-to-sales ratio of approximately 80x, which the host acknowledges is higher than virtually any company at this scale in history. However, he argues the market is pricing platform optionality rather than current revenue. Revenue projections for 2026 alone are cited as $22–24 billion. ARK Invest's model reportedly projects a $2.5 trillion enterprise value by 2030, with a bull case of $3.1 trillion, suggesting the $2 trillion IPO price is a discount to those independent projections. Dan Ives at Wedbush Securities described a potential Tesla–SpaceX combination as "the holy grail for controlling the entire AI infrastructure across Earth and space."

5. **Ripple effects across the space industry:** When SpaceX's IPO news broke, Rocket Lab stock reportedly jumped 5.5%, Planet Labs jumped 11%, and Intuitive Machines jumped over 10%, as institutional investors interpreted the filing as validation of the broader space economy as an investable sector. The host notes that Boeing, which once built the Saturn V rocket, now has a space division that is "essentially a rounding error" compared to SpaceX. A public SpaceX with $50–75 billion in fresh capital would accelerate the competitive gap further.

6. **Tesla–SpaceX merger speculation:** Chamath Palihapitiya, early Facebook executive and host of the All-In podcast, stated in January 2026 that he believes SpaceX will not IPO independently but will instead reverse-merge into Tesla, with Elon Musk using the moment to consolidate control of his two flagship assets into one cap table. He described the potential combined entity as "the Berkshire Hathaway of the modern century." Polymarket prediction markets have placed odds of a Tesla–SpaceX merger at 6–18% depending on the time frame. SpaceX president and COO Gwynne Shotwell was quoted in Time magazine saying she is "looking forward" to the IPO, calling it "a new thing, a new set of methodologies to run companies."

7. **Terafab as convergence infrastructure:** In March 2026, Tesla, SpaceX, and XAI jointly announced Terafab, a $25 billion advanced chip fabrication complex in Austin, Texas, targeting 100,000 wafer starts per month and scaling to one million. The facility is designed to build custom AI chips and memory chips for self-driving cars, humanoid robots, and AI computing satellites in space. Elon Musk stated that current global chip production meets only 2–3% of what his companies will need in the future. The host notes that 80% of Terafab's compute output is targeted at space and orbital applications. XAI was also acquired by SpaceX in an all-stock deal in February 2026.

## Financials

1. **SpaceX financials in 2025:** SpaceX generated approximately $15–16 billion in total revenue in 2025, with Starlink contributing an estimated $10–12 billion of that. The company generated roughly $8 billion in profit. For 2026, revenue projections are cited at $22–24 billion. The IPO is targeting a raise of $50–75 billion at a $2 trillion valuation.

2. **Starship cost economics:** Falcon 9 currently costs customers approximately $2,700–$3,000 per kilogram to orbit. Starship's aspirational target is $10–$100 per kilogram, representing a 30–300x reduction from current Falcon 9 prices and over a 1,000x reduction from industry prices a decade ago. The vehicle itself costs approximately $90 million to build, but with propellant and operations costs of roughly $500,000–$2 million per launch, and assuming 100 flights per vehicle, the cost per flight is estimated at approximately $2–5 million for 100–150 tons of payload, equating to roughly $33 per kilogram.
