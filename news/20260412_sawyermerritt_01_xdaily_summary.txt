## Key Posts / Themes

1. **FSD (Supervised) Launches in the Netherlands:** In two posts (garnering a combined ~5.9K likes and 509 reposts), Sawyer Merritt reported that Tesla FSD (Supervised) is officially rolling out to customer vehicles in the Netherlands — marking a notable European market expansion. Notably, Tesla is requiring all Dutch owners activating FSD for the first time to complete a short educational quiz confirming they understand how the system operates before it engages. Tesla published a dedicated video explaining FSD's functionality for this rollout. This appears to be the first confirmed European retail customer deployment of FSD (Supervised), representing a regulatory and geographic milestone.

2. **Cybercab Validation Testing Expands to Kansas:** A post with 2.6K likes and 242 reposts flagged a Cybercab spotted testing in Wichita, Kansas — described as the first time a Cybercab has been seen in that state. Merritt noted that Tesla has recently produced significantly more engineering and validation Cybercabs, and suggested investors should expect sightings in many new geographic areas as the validation program broadens. This is consistent with a pre-production engineering validation push ahead of a manufacturing ramp, and the geographic spread of testing units is a qualitative signal of program progress.

3. **Tesla Announces Signature Edition Model S and Model X as Final Farewell:** Tesla announced limited "Signature Edition" versions of the Model S and Model X, framed explicitly as a final goodbye for both vehicle lines. Priced at $159,420, only 250 Model S and 100 Model X units will be produced, all in Plaid configuration with a Garnet Red exterior paint and matching door handles. The strict production caps (350 total units) and the "farewell" framing confirm Tesla is formally retiring these two flagship sedans/SUVs, likely to redirect resources toward next-generation platforms.

4. **SpaceX Starlink Chip Development (Low Tesla Relevance):** Merritt noted a SpaceX job posting hinting at development of compact multi-chip modules for Starlink Mobile radio frequency handling. This has no direct Tesla investment relevance and is included only for completeness; it was skipped for deeper analysis.
