## Key Posts / Themes

1. **FSD (Supervised) Receives First European Approval — Netherlands Launches at €99/Month:** In the day's most significant development, Tesla announced that FSD (Supervised) has been officially approved by the Dutch regulatory authority (RDW) and will begin rolling out to customer vehicles in the Netherlands shortly. FSD subscriptions launched at €99/month, making this the first European country to receive regulatory clearance. Merritt emphasized this clears the path for approvals in other European countries and potentially an EU-wide rollout. The RDW issued a notably strong official statement, describing the system as "safer than other driver assistance systems" due to "continuous strict monitoring of the driver," and stating that "proper use of this driver's system makes a positive contribution to road safety." This regulatory endorsement from an EU-member authority carries significant weight as a template for broader European expansion. Dutch Tesla owners will go from Autopilot directly to FSD V14, which Merritt called a generational leap in capability overnight. This post reached 3.9K likes and 477 retweets, indicating high viral reach on a material claim.

2. **FSD Fleet Activity Surges to 19.2 Million Miles Per Day:** Tesla updated its FSD miles tracker, reflecting both a larger vehicle fleet and increased FSD usage. The fleet is now averaging 19.2 million miles per day on FSD — up from 14.4 million miles per day just a couple of months ago, representing approximately a 33% increase. Merritt noted this equates to 1,000 miles every 4.5 seconds. This accelerating data accumulation is a key input for model training velocity and a direct signal of FSD adoption growth, reinforcing the autonomous driving thesis.

3. **Robotaxi App Updated with Safety and Operational Features:** Tesla pushed an update to its Robotaxi app, adding a new tips section that informs riders how many seats are available, explains that vehicle lights will pulse upon arrival, and discloses that Tesla may use the interior cabin camera for safety monitoring. The app also received a darker visual appearance. Separately, Merritt flagged that Tesla has begun using cabin cameras to assess driver age — a feature he speculated could be used to prevent unaccompanied minors from hailing autonomous robotaxi rides. Together, these updates suggest Tesla is actively refining the operational and safety infrastructure for its robotaxi service ahead of broader deployment.

4. **Model Y Tops China Sales Charts in March 2026; New Market Launches:** The Tesla Model Y was the top-selling passenger vehicle in China in March 2026, with 39,827 retail registrations — beating all EVs and ICE models across sedans, SUVs, and MPVs. Merritt highlighted it was the only premium-priced model in the top ten, underscoring its competitive positioning. On the product front, Tesla officially launched the larger Model Y L in Israel, with first deliveries starting in June. Tesla also discontinued the white interior on all Model Y Premium trims built at Giga Shanghai (affecting Australia, New Zealand, South Korea, Japan, Philippines, and other markets), replacing it with a new Zen Grey interior. South Korea saw a 7.7% price increase on the Model Y L.

5. **Tesla Semi Factory on the Cusp of First Assembly Production:** A full factory tour of the Tesla Semi facility revealed a quote from Tesla's Dan Priestley: "We're right on the cusp of starting to produce first assemblies off these lines." This suggests Semi production is imminent, though no specific production date or volume target was stated.
