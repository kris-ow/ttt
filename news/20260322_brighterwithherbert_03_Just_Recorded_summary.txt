This video covers a major announcement by Elon Musk — referred to internally as the "Terafab" project — a joint initiative between Tesla, xAI, and SpaceX to build an advanced semiconductor fabrication facility in Austin, Texas, aimed at producing a terawatt of AI compute. The core thesis is that existing global chip supply (approximately 20 gigawatts per year) represents only about 2% of what is needed for this vision, making it necessary to build an entirely new class of chip manufacturing infrastructure. The broader ambition frames this as a civilizational-scale project toward becoming a multi-planetary, eventually galactic, species powered by space-based solar energy and AI.

## Humanoid Bots
1. Elon Musk stated that humanoid robot production — specifically Optimus — is expected to reach between 1 billion and 10 billion units per year at scale, compared to approximately 100 million vehicles produced globally per year. He noted that humanoid robots will be made "10 to 100 times more than the volume of cars," and that Tesla's goal is to produce a very significant percentage of those units. This positions Optimus as Tesla's largest long-term volume product by far.

2. One of the two primary chip types to be manufactured at the Terafab will be optimized for edge inference, intended primarily for use in Optimus robots. Because of the projected volume differential between humanoid robots and cars, Optimus is described as the dominant driver of this chip category's demand. The chip will also serve Tesla vehicles but Optimus is the primary focus.

## Market & Competition
1. Musk announced the "Terafab" — a joint advanced semiconductor fabrication facility being built in Austin, Texas, in collaboration between Tesla, xAI, and SpaceX. The facility is described as unique in that it will house all equipment necessary to manufacture chips (logic and memory), create lithography masks, package, and test chips within a single building, enabling a rapid recursive design loop. Musk claimed this integrated capability does not currently exist anywhere else in the world, and estimated their recursive improvement rate would be "probably an order of magnitude better than anything else in the world."

2. The current global AI compute output is approximately 20 gigawatts per year, and the combined output of all semiconductor fabs on Earth represents only about 2% of what the Terafab project requires (targeting 1 terawatt). Musk stated he has personally told existing suppliers — including Samsung, TSMC, and Micron — to expand as fast as possible and that Tesla/xAI will buy all their chips, but that their maximum expansion rate is insufficient to meet demand. This framing positions the Terafab as a necessity, not a preference.

3. A second chip type to be developed at Terafab will be optimized for space-based deployment — designed to handle high-energy ions, photons, electron buildup, and higher operating temperatures to minimize radiator mass. Musk projected that space-based compute will ultimately dwarf terrestrial compute, estimating approximately 100–200 gigawatts per year in terrestrial chips versus roughly 1 terawatt of chips deployed in space, driven by the superior and uninterrupted solar power availability in orbit (at least 5x more solar power than ground-based systems, with no atmospheric attenuation, day/night cycles, or seasonality).

4. Musk argued that within 2–3 years, it will be cheaper to deploy AI compute in space than on the ground, citing lower solar panel costs (no heavy glass or framing needed), no battery requirements, and increasing economies of scale in space versus increasing difficulty and cost of ground-based power expansion due to land scarcity and NIMBY opposition. Starship is identified as the critical enabler, with a target of 10 million tons to orbit per year; the current Starship B3 can carry 100 tons to orbit, with Starship V4 expected to carry 200 tons.
