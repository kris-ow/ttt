This Friday news roundup from March 27, 2026 covers a broad mix of Tesla-related and broader automotive/tech news, including Tesla's Terafab chip initiative, Cybercab drivetrain confirmation, Q1 delivery expectations, Uber's robotaxi partnership strategy, SpaceX's potential IPO, and the competitive struggles of legacy automakers. ## Electric Vehicles

1. **Q1 2026 Delivery Consensus Published Early:** Tesla has published its Q1 2026 delivery consensus, which the host notes came out unusually early and was previously harder to find (sometimes only emailed to investor relations subscribers). The consensus figure is approximately 365,000 deliveries, compared to 418,000 in Q4 2025. The host believes there may be an upside surprise but cautions that beating the number doesn't guarantee a stock price increase given current market conditions.

2. **Mystery Car Body Spotted in Texas — Likely Model Y L:** A car body shipped in a crate was spotted in Texas, generating speculation. The host is confident this is the Model Y L (a larger/refreshed Model Y variant), dismissing alternative theories about shape and hatch angles as a "popsicle illusion" caused by an unusual top three-quarter viewing angle and a piece of plastic disguising the contours. The host notes it was likely shipped from Berlin, Shanghai, or Fremont. He had previously predicted the Model Y L would arrive in the first half of 2026, likely Q1, and considers this a confirmation of that prediction.

3. **Model S and X Likely Discontinued:** The host states that when he saw the Q4 "other models" figure of approximately 11,600 units, he concluded the Model S and X were done. He notes that a large area under construction next to the S/X line at the factory appeared to be for Optimus production, and that the announcement of discontinuation came roughly two weeks after his prediction. He adds that the Model Y L will partially satisfy demand that previously went to the Model X.

4. **Toyota Investing $1 Billion in US EV Production:** Toyota's outgoing CEO Koji Sato warned at a supplier summit that the company is "battling for survival," citing pressure from Chinese automakers and the increasing importance of software. Toyota is investing $800 million at its Georgetown, Kentucky plant to manufacture a second EV domestically, with total investment across Kentucky and Indiana plants reaching $1 billion. The host notes a friend's experience with a new Toyota Highlander highlights Toyota's software shortcomings as a key vulnerability.

5. **Model Y Number One in UK for the Quarter:** The host references recent data showing the Model Y reached number one in the UK for the quarter, attributing the earlier apparent sales slump to shipping logistics — ships carrying vehicles had simply not yet arrived, making sales figures temporarily misleading.

## Robotaxi

1. **Cybercab Confirmed as Front-Wheel Drive:** The host states that the Cybercab being front-wheel drive is not a rumor but a confirmed fact, having spoken with the engineers who built it approximately 17 months prior. The rationale for front-wheel drive includes: a shorter wiring harness, sufficient pull at the speeds the Cybercab will operate, slightly better regenerative braking, a smaller cooling loop, and compatibility with the Unboxed manufacturing process. The host had initially expected rear-wheel drive based on earlier engineer conversations but says the builders themselves confirmed front-wheel drive.

2. **Robotaxi Deployment Slower Than Expected:** The host acknowledges that robotaxi deployment is behind expectations, noting that a quarter of 2026 has already passed without the breadth of deployments the market was anticipating. He suggests it will be at least a year before robotaxi contributes materially to financials. He discusses the importance of an upcoming FSD version (referred to as "14.3"), suggesting that if it meaningfully improves performance, it could allow the ratio of remote supervisors to robotaxis to improve — for example, going from 3:1 to 4:1 would allow a one-third fleet increase, and 10:1 would allow tripling the fleet.

3. **California Classifies Tesla Robotaxis as Limo Service:** California regulators have classified Tesla's robotaxis as a chauffeur/limo service rather than an autonomous vehicle service, which means Tesla avoids many mandatory reporting requirements — including disengagement data — that apply to traditional autonomous vehicle permits.

4. **Uber's "Deal Blitz" to Prevent Robotaxi Monopoly:** Uber has partnered with at least a dozen robotaxi players in recent years, with three new partnerships announced in recent weeks: Zoox, Wave Nissan, and Rivian. Previous partnerships include Waymo, AVride, May Mobility, Momenta, Pony AI, Wave, BYD's Apollo Go, Motional, and Nuro. The host is skeptical of this strategy, arguing that Uber owns neither its cars, drivers, nor customers, and that customer loyalty is driven purely by price. He contrasts this with Tesla's vertically integrated approach and dismisses the Nvidia/Lucid/Uber "stack" narrative, noting that Lucid does not have a self-driving car.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Version 14.3 as a Key Scaling Lever:** The host expresses anticipation for an upcoming FSD version he refers to as "14.3," suggesting that if it delivers meaningful performance improvements, it could significantly reduce the supervisor-to-vehicle ratio required for safe robotaxi operation, enabling rapid fleet scaling without proportional increases in staffing.

## Financials

1. **Tesla Semi Expected Gross Margin Positive from Day One:** The host says he is confident the Tesla Semi will be gross margin positive from its first day of production but notes that volumes will be too low in 2026 to be material relative to Tesla's overall scale.

2. **Megapack Revenue Has a Long Flywheel:** The host highlights Megapack as a strong contributor to margins and revenue over the coming years. He explains that Tesla doesn't receive full payment until units are energized/deployed, meaning revenue from already-manufactured units will continue flowing in for potentially one and a half to two years even if production stopped today. He expects quarters where deployments exceed current production due to this backlog dynamic.

3. **Ford CEO Jim Farley Paid $27.5 Million Amid Billion-Dollar Losses:** The host highlights that Ford paid CEO Jim Farley $27.5 million in the prior year despite writing down billions in losses, calling it emblematic of misaligned executive incentive structures at legacy automakers.

4. **Margin Compression Expected to Continue:** Responding to a viewer question, the host agrees that margin compression is likely to continue through 2026, with robotaxi not expected to contribute meaningfully for at least another year.

## Market & Competition

1. **VW's Skoda Brand Lost 96% of China Sales:** Volkswagen's Skoda brand sold 341,000 units in China in 2018; that figure has collapsed to approximately 15,000, a 96% decline. The host attributes this to Chinese brands being both cheaper and more desirable, with Skoda offering little brand cachet. He notes this as an example of the broader existential threat facing legacy automakers in China.

2. **VW Partners with Xpeng for Software in China and Southeast Asia:** Volkswagen is partnering with Xpeng to integrate their software into VW vehicles, starting in China and Southeast Asia with hopes to expand. The first vehicle with Xpeng software is the ID. Unyx 08, described as having a distinctly Chinese design aesthetic. The host notes VW is separately partnered with Rivian for software in Western markets.

3. **Tesla Terafab Chip Initiative — Serious Salaries Signal Real Commitment:** Business Insider reported on Tesla's hiring for the Terafab chip project, revealing compensation ranges: $88,000–$240,000 for the lithography team (requiring ~10 years of experience), and up to $338,000 for integration engineers building advanced logistics chips. The host interprets these salaries as confirmation that Tesla is seriously committed to building its own chip manufacturing capability. He notes that Tesla needs a volume of chips that does not currently exist in the market, and that xAI and SpaceX will be partners in the Terafab endeavor.

4. **SpaceX IPO May Come Between Now and June 2026:** Analysis from "Tesla Boomer Mama" on X suggests a SpaceX IPO could arrive between late March and June 2026. Valuation estimates range from $1.25 trillion on the low end to $1.75 trillion, with a possible bump to $2 trillion in the days following the IPO. A 5x float multiplier may apply for stocks with under 20% free float. The IPO may allocate approximately 30% to retail investors, roughly three times the usual amount. The host says he would consider liquidating other holdings to buy SpaceX shares.

5. **Amazon/Blue Origin Files for 51,000 Compute Satellites:** Days after Amazon asked the FCC to reject SpaceX's application for space-based data centers, Blue Origin filed a request for 51,000 data center satellites of its own. The host notes the irony, pointing out that Blue Origin lacks a sufficiently capable launch vehicle to deploy such a constellation cost-effectively.

6. **Federal Regulators Reject Recall Petition for Tesla One-Pedal Driving:** US federal regulators have denied a petition to recall Tesla vehicles over one-pedal driving (regenerative braking), ruling the system is working as intended. The host supports this outcome, comparing regenerative braking to downshifting in a manual transmission.
