This Monday news roundup from the Futurazza channel, hosted by Brian, covers a wide range of Tesla-related stories from February 16th. The episode touches on Cybercab hiring activity, Grok's European rollout, current Tesla financing deals, the Buffalo factory situation, and competitive EV discounts from rival brands. Brian's overall tone is cautiously optimistic on Tesla while pushing back on what he sees as disproportionate negative coverage.

1. Cybercab hiring signals serious production intent. Tesla is actively recruiting injection molding engineers, process engineers, maintenance supervisors, and equipment technicians specifically for the Cybercab. Brian argues this kind of targeted technical hiring is not something a company does for a vaporware product, suggesting production ramp-up is genuinely imminent.

2. Grok is rolling out to Tesla vehicles in Europe. The AI assistant is launching in the UK, Ireland, Germany, Switzerland, Austria, Italy, France, Portugal, and Spain, where it can answer real-time questions and add navigation destinations. Brian notes he's made a separate video demonstrating Grok's utility as a personal tour guide, calling it "remarkably good" for a use case it wasn't specifically designed for. He couldn't explain why other European markets were excluded, speculating it may be regulatory.

3. Model Y continues to outsell EV rivals in Europe despite headwinds. Brian addressed criticism from X users with limited account history claiming Tesla is "finished" in Europe, attributing the negativity to bad-faith accounts. He acknowledges real headwinds — arriving competition, limited model selection, and brand damage — but argues Tesla's bigger strategic focus is autonomy, not raw car sales volume, and that FSD coming to Europe will be a significant sales catalyst.

4. Tesla executive Raj departed after 13 years. He had been running Tesla's North American sales team following Troy Jones. Brian downplays the departure, noting 13 years at that level is a long tenure, that Tesla is a notoriously demanding workplace, and that executive turnover there is not as high as critics suggest.

5. Tesla launched a $450 bottle of Mezcal under the Mezcow brand. Fifty randomly assigned boxes are personally signed by founders Verdura and Von Holtzen. The bottle remains in stock at time of recording. Brian's main commentary is that the price point, not the tasting notes (floral, delicate, spicy with hints of lime, green apple, and spice), is what most people will fixate on.

6. FSD is now subscription-only in the US, with the Lux package as the primary exception. February 14th was the last day to purchase FSD outright. The Lux package bundles FSD supervised, unlimited supercharging, four-year premium service (wheel, tire, windshield protection, recommended maintenance), but critically the FSD does not transfer with the vehicle upon resale — it ends with the owner's tenure. Brian expresses mild frustration with this structure, particularly with unlimited supercharging giveaways, which he says effectively raises costs for paying customers since superchargers are designed to break even, not profit.

7. Tesla's current financing deals are competitive but not extraordinary. Highlights include 0.99% APR on Model 3, Model Y leases from $459/month at 0% APR, and Cybertruck financing at 3.99%. Additional perks include $500 off for veterans, first responders, healthcare workers, teachers, and students; 2,000 free supercharging miles for EV trade-ins; and a free FSD transfer through end of month for existing FSD owners buying a new Tesla.

8. Competitor EV discounts dwarf Tesla's offers and deserve equal scrutiny. The 2026 Chevy Equinox is being discounted $6,500 to $10,000 depending on trade-in vehicle. Polestar 3 is offering $18,000 off plus 2.99% financing. Kia EV6 has $5,000 off plus 0% interest, and the 2026 Hyundai Ioniq 5 is $5,000 off plus 0% over 72 months. Brian notes Q1 is always the weakest sales quarter industry-wide and questions why only Tesla gets accused of desperation during promotional periods.

9. Tesla's Buffalo factory has met New York State job requirements. Despite legislative attempts to claw back subsidies and revoke dealership licenses — motivated in part by anger over Elon Musk's DOGE involvement — Tesla reported 2,399 full-time employees at the South Buffalo facility at the end of 2025, plus roughly 1,000 more at other New York State Tesla locations including dealerships. Tesla has also invested $350 million in supercomputers at the site and has begun manufacturing solar panels. Brian notes the original deal was struck with Solar City, not Tesla, and that Tesla honored it after the acquisition.

10. China tech developments worth watching. CATL announced a new 5C battery rated for one million miles even under ultra-fast charging conditions. Chery Automobile announced the world's first mass-produced fully electronic mechanical braking system (brake-by-wire, no hydraulics), which Brian expects to appear on the Cybercab. Pony AI and GAC Toyota also debuted a mass-produced robotaxi based on the Volkswagen ID. Buzz platform. Brian plans to visit Guangzhou in coming weeks to personally observe operational robotaxi services and tour the Xpeng factory.
