Electrek reports that Tesla's "Robotaxi" expansion into Dallas and Houston — announced days before Q1 2026 earnings — has shown near-zero vehicle availability, with data suggesting only one car deployed per city, prompting the author to characterize it as a pre-earnings stock narrative rather than a genuine operational expansion.

## Robotaxi
1. **Dallas and Houston launch is effectively symbolic:** Data from Robotaxi Tracker shows 0–2% availability in both new markets over the first 24 hours, with only brief spikes to around 50% during a narrow morning window before dropping back to zero. Only a single vehicle has been reported by riders in each market. The geofenced zones are small — roughly 12–15 square miles in Houston (Jersey Village and Willowbrook areas) and 30–35 square miles in Dallas — compared to Houston's metro area of over 10,000 square miles.

2. **Austin operations remain limited after 10 months:** Tesla has approximately a dozen unsupervised vehicles in Austin and still relies heavily on safety monitors in most rides, despite Austin's expanded 245-square-mile geofence. The author argues that if Tesla were confident in the technology, it would be scaling Austin rather than spreading thin across new cities.

## Autonomous Driving
3. **Safety record and incident data:** Tesla has reported 15 crashes to NHTSA since launching in Austin, which the article characterizes as a crash rate roughly 4x worse than human drivers. The article notes that Tesla — along with Waymo, Zoox, Aurora, and Nuro — redacts detailed crash narratives from the NHTSA database, though the author singles out Tesla's safety record as particularly concerning. Early incidents in Dallas and Houston have already been captured on social media, including a vehicle incorrectly navigating onto a freeway.

## Financials
4. **Q1 2026 earnings context and valuation:** Tesla's Q1 2026 earnings are due Wednesday, April 22. Q1 deliveries came in at 358,023 vehicles, below analyst consensus and down from 418,227 in Q4 2025. Analyst EPS estimates range from $0.24 to $0.40. Tesla stock trades around $400, approximately flat year-to-date, at roughly 178x forward earnings versus 8–12x for the broader auto industry.

## Editorial Stance
The author argues this is the third instance of Tesla making a splashy autonomous driving announcement timed precisely before an earnings call — following a one-time "driverless delivery" in June 2025 and "unsupervised" rides that disappeared within a week in January 2026 — and that Tesla is expanding the *narrative* of a robotaxi service rather than actual scaled operations, with safety as the binding constraint.
