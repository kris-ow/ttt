This video is Tesla's official Q4 and Full Year 2025 Financial Results and Q&A Webcast, featuring opening remarks from Elon Musk and CFO Vaibhav Taneja, followed by analyst and investor Q&A. The call covers Tesla's financial results for Q4 2025, outlines a massively expanded 2026 capital expenditure plan exceeding $20 billion, and details Tesla's strategic pivot toward autonomous vehicles, robotaxi deployment, Optimus humanoid robots, energy, and semiconductor self-sufficiency. The central thesis is that Tesla is transitioning from a car company into an autonomous transportation and robotics platform, with 2026 described as the opening of "a new book" in the company's history.

## Financials
1. **Q4 2025 Automotive Margins Recover Sequentially:** Automotive gross margins excluding credits improved from 15.4% to 17.9% sequentially in Q4 2025. Automotive gross profit was roughly flat sequentially despite 16% lower deliveries, primarily due to a favorable regional mix with proportionately more deliveries in APAC and AMIA regions.

2. **Total Gross Margin Exceeds 20% for First Time in Two Years:** Tesla ended Q4 2025 with total gross margin above 20.1%, a level not achieved in over two years. This improvement came despite lower fixed-cost absorption and tariff headwinds that exceeded $500 million in Q4.

3. **Energy Segment Achieves Record Revenue and Gross Profit:** The energy segment hit another record gross profit in Q4 and ended the full year 2025 with nearly $12.88 billion in revenue, representing 26.6% year-over-year growth, driven by higher Megapack and Powerwall deployments across all regions.

4. **Free Cash Flow of $1.4 Billion in Q4:** Tesla generated $1.4 billion in free cash flow in Q4 2025. Capital expenditures came in slightly below the prior guidance of $9 billion for the full year 2025.

5. **2026 Capex Guidance Exceeds $20 Billion:** Tesla guided for capital expenditures in excess of $20 billion for 2026, covering six new factories (a refinery, LFP factory, Cybercab factory, Semi factory, a new Megafactory, and an Optimus factory), expanded AI compute infrastructure, existing factory capacity expansion, robotaxi fleet expansion, and Optimus fleet expansion. This figure does not include any potential investments in solar cell manufacturing or a semiconductor fab (Terafab), which are still in early phases.

6. **Cash and Investments Stand at Over $44 Billion:** Tesla noted it has over $44 billion in cash and investments on its balance sheet to fund near-term capex needs. Management indicated it has had conversations with banks about financing the robotaxi fleet given its predictable cash flow profile, and will consider debt or other means for longer-duration infrastructure investments.

7. **Net Income Impacted by Bitcoin Mark-to-Market and FX:** Net income in Q4 was negatively impacted by mark-to-market charges on Bitcoin holdings, which depreciated 23% compared to the prior quarter, and by unfavorable foreign exchange impacts from large intercompany positions.

8. **Operating Expenses Rose on Stock Compensation and AI/New Product Spend:** Operating expenses increased sequentially primarily due to higher stock-based compensation for employees, charges related to a 2025 CEO performance award deemed probable over the near term, and continued elevated spending on AI initiatives and new products including Cybercab, Semi, Optimus, and Megapack. Management expects this elevated spend to continue throughout 2026.

9. **FSD Transitioning Fully to Subscription Model:** FSD adoption reached nearly 1.1 million paid customers globally, with nearly 70% having made upfront purchases. Beginning this quarter, Tesla is transitioning fully to a subscription-based model for FSD, meaning net additions will primarily come via subscription, which will negatively impact automotive margins in the short term.

10. **Services and Other Margin Declined:** Services and other gross margin declined from 10.5% to 8.8%, primarily from higher employee-related costs at service centers as Tesla prepares for fleet growth. Supercharging margins improved within this segment. Robotaxi-related costs are also captured within this segment but are not material at this stage; revenue and cost-per-mile metrics are described as not yet meaningful to discuss.

11. **Tesla Investment in xAI Announced:** Tesla disclosed an investment in xAI as a strategic initiative, framed as a furtherance of Master Plan 4. Grok is already being used in Tesla vehicles. The rationale includes using Grok to optimize management of large autonomous fleets and, potentially, to orchestrate large teams of Optimus robots.

## Autonomous Driving
1. **Unsupervised FSD Live in Austin With No Chase Car:** Tesla has launched paid robotaxi rides in Austin with no safety monitor in the vehicle and, as of approximately the day before the call, with no chase car following the vehicle either. Elon Musk emphasized Tesla is being very cautious to ensure no injuries or serious accidents during the rollout, proceeding intersection by intersection and city by city.

2. **FSD V14 Delivered Major Performance Jump to Customer Vehicles:** A variant of the software used for the robotaxi service was shipped to customers as V14, resulting in what was described as a "huge jump in performance" with strong positive feedback. The software has continued to improve since that release, with customers reportedly eager to have driver monitoring requirements removed.

3. **Nationwide FSD Rollout Target for 2026:** Elon Musk stated Tesla expects to have fully autonomous vehicles operating in somewhere between a quarter and half of the United States by end of 2026, pending regulatory approvals. In the absence of federal preemption legislation, Tesla will proceed on a city-by-city and state-by-state basis, targeting dozens of major cities by year-end.

4. **Federal Preemption Could Accelerate Expansion:** Musk noted that federal preemption for autonomous vehicles would be a significant positive catalyst, removing the need to navigate individual city and state regulatory processes. Without it, the expansion will still proceed but more granularly.

5. **Tesla AI Claims Order-of-Magnitude Memory Efficiency Lead:** Elon Musk asserted that Tesla's AI is more than an order of magnitude more memory-efficient (intelligence per gigabyte) than competing large models from other companies, positioning Tesla favorably for scaling constrained by memory supply.

## Robotaxi
1. **Robotaxi Fleet Exceeds 500 Vehicles Across Austin and Bay Area:** Tesla confirmed that robotaxi vehicles carrying paid customers number well over 500 across the Bay Area and Austin combined. Fleet size varies by time of day, with more vehicles deployed during peak hours. Musk suggested the fleet size would roughly double every month on an exponential growth curve.

2. **Cybercab Production Expected to Start in April 2026:** Elon Musk stated Tesla expects to begin Cybercab production in April 2026. He described a typical S-curve ramp: starting slowly, then growing exponentially, before plateauing at a target volume. Cybercab has no steering wheel or pedals, making autonomy a hard requirement, not a fallback.

3. **Cybercab Designed for High Utilization and Durability:** The Cybercab was designed to optimize fully considered cost per mile for autonomous operation. Elon Musk estimated it would be used approximately 50 to 60 hours per week, compared to roughly 10 to 11 hours per week for a personally owned driven vehicle. He described the design philosophy as closer to a commercial truck built for near-continuous operation.

4. **Personal Tesla Fleet-Sharing Network Planned (Airbnb Model):** Tesla plans to allow existing Tesla owners with AI4-equipped vehicles to add or remove their cars from the autonomous fleet, similar to the Airbnb model. Musk suggested the earnings potential from lending a car to the fleet could exceed the owner's lease cost, effectively meaning owners could be paid to own a Tesla. He noted this potential is underappreciated given the millions of AI4-equipped Teslas already on the road.

5. **Long-Term Vision: Cybercab to Dwarf All Other Tesla Vehicle Production:** Musk projected that long-term, Cybercab production will be several times greater than all other Tesla vehicles combined, based on the statistic that approximately 90% of vehicle miles traveled involve one or two occupants, and roughly 80% involve just one occupant.

6. **Robotaxi Infrastructure Already in Place via Supercharger and Service Network:** Tesla executives emphasized that the existing Supercharger and service center network gives Tesla a unique infrastructure advantage for scaling the robotaxi fleet — an advantage no competitor currently possesses. This infrastructure will be augmented as fleet density grows in specific cities.

## Humanoid Bots
1. **Model S and X Production to End Next Quarter; Fremont Space Converted to Optimus Factory:** Elon Musk announced that Model S and Model X production will wind down and stop next quarter (Q1 2026). The production space in the Fremont factory previously occupied by Model S and X will be converted into an Optimus manufacturing line, with a long-term goal of producing 1 million Optimus units per year from that facility.

2. **Optimus Gen 3 Reveal Expected in a Few Months:** Musk stated Tesla will "probably" unveil Optimus Gen 3 in a few months. He described it as an "incredibly capable robot" that looks sufficiently human-like that people could be confused about whether it is human, which also supports the AI learning strategy by enabling imitation of human task demonstrations.

3. **Optimus Gen 3 Learns Tasks from Demonstrations, Verbal Instructions, and Video:** Musk described Optimus Gen 3 as capable of learning new tasks by observing human behavior, receiving verbal instructions, watching demonstrations, or viewing video. He characterized it as a general-purpose robot.

4. **Significant Optimus Production Volume Not Expected Until End of 2026:** Musk stated Tesla would not expect any meaningful Optimus production volume until probably the end of 2026. Current units are still in an R&D phase; older versions are deprecated as new ones are developed, and current factory deployment is not material.

5. **Three Hardest Challenges in Humanoid Robotics Identified:** Musk outlined three core engineering challenges for humanoid robots: (1) building a hand with human-equivalent degrees of freedom and dexterity, (2) developing real-world AI, and (3) scaling production. He claimed Tesla is the only company currently addressing all three.

6. **China Identified as Primary Competitive Threat in Humanoid Robots:** Musk stated China will be by far the toughest competition for Optimus, citing China's world-class manufacturing scale and improving AI capabilities. He said Tesla sees no significant humanoid robot competitors outside of China.

## Energy
1. **Energy Segment Sees Record Deployments and Revenue in 2025:** The energy segment set another deployment record in Q4 2025 and achieved nearly $12.88 billion in full-year 2025 revenue, up 26.6% year-over-year, with strong demand for both Megapack and Powerwall.

2. **Megapack 3 and Mega Block to Launch in 2026:** Management cited the upcoming launch of Megapack 3 and Mega Block as drivers of increasing deployments in 2026. However, margin compression is expected from low-cost competition, policy uncertainty, and tariff costs.

3. **Backlog Remains Strong and Globally Diversified:** The energy segment enters 2026 with a strong, well-diversified global backlog. Management expressed high confidence in continued growth "as far into the future as we can imagine."

4. **Solar Cell Manufacturing a Major New Strategic Initiative:** Elon Musk outlined a goal of reaching 100 gigawatts per year of solar cell production, with full vertical integration from raw materials to finished solar panels. Tesla views solar combined with batteries as the best solution for powering AI data centers, both ground-based and potentially space-based. This initiative is in early stages and not included in the $20 billion+ capex figure.

## Electric Vehicles
1. **Q4 Deliveries Down 16% Sequentially but Backlog Grew:** Q4 2025 deliveries were 16% lower than Q3 2025, partially because the Q3 IRA consumer tax credit deadline pulled forward demand from Q4 in the US. Internationally, record deliveries were achieved in Malaysia, Norway, Poland, Saudi Arabia, and Taiwan, with continued strength across APAC and AMIA, resulting in a larger year-end backlog than in recent years.

2. **Battery Pack Supply Remains the Primary Production Constraint:** Management cited battery packs as the biggest constraint to production globally. Teams have been working around this by placing 4680 cells in non-structural packs as a creative near-term solution while continuing to iterate on improvements.

3. **Least Expensive Tesla Models Ever Recently Launched:** Management confirmed Tesla has launched its lowest-priced models ever over recent months and is continuing to expand these globally, consistent with the long-standing strategy of reducing vehicle cost without sacrificing range or performance.

4. **Next-Generation Roadster Debut Targeted for April 2026:** Elon Musk mentioned the next-generation Roadster, describing it as "something out of this world," with a hoped-for April 2026 debut.

5. **Cybertruck Leads the Electric Truck Segment:** Cybertruck was described as the best-selling electric truck in its segment, outselling all electric truck competitors while competitors have been pulling back. The Cybertruck line was noted as Tesla's most fully autonomy-ready platform and is expected to eventually be transitioned to a fully autonomous cargo delivery use case.

## Market & Competition
1. **Autonomous Vehicles Expected to Displace Most Human-Driven Miles:** Musk projected that less than 5% of vehicle miles traveled in the future will involve a human driver, possibly as low as 1%, framing the long-term market as dominated by autonomous transportation-as-a-service rather than personal vehicle ownership.

2. **Tesla Building Domestic Chip and Memory Supply Chain to Mitigate Geopolitical Risk:** Elon Musk stated Tesla has chip supply agreements with TSMC in Arizona and Samsung in Texas, but noted there are currently zero advanced memory fabs at scale in the United States. He argued Tesla must build a "Terafab" — a large-scale domestic fab integrating logic, memory, and packaging — to prevent being supply-constrained in three to four years, particularly under adverse geopolitical scenarios.

3. **Tesla Claims World's Most Advanced Lithium and Cathode Refineries:** Musk stated Tesla's lithium refinery in Corpus Christi and cathode refinery in Austin use entirely new processes that are more advanced and efficient than any others in the world. He noted Tesla is essentially the only company operating lithium and cathode refineries in America, and expressed frustration that no other companies have built similar facilities.
