A YouTuber named Remmy Evans purchased a heavily stripped Tesla Model 3 rolling chassis for $2,000 and drove, drifted, and jumped it — with the battery still displaying 212 miles of range on a full charge.

## Electric Vehicles
1. **The vehicle and its condition:** The Model 3 had been stripped to a bare chassis — no body panels, windshield, or seatbelts — by a previous owner named Grayson, who had purchased it for $6,000–$7,000 intending to transplant the drivetrain into a 1970s concept car. The project was abandoned after an estimated 800 hours of bodywork was deemed too burdensome. Evans negotiated the price down from $3,000 to $2,000. The car had been sitting unregistered for at least two years.

2. **Battery and software state:** After new tires were fitted, the car still showed 212 miles of range at full charge. However, the software was throwing 78 error codes due to missing cameras, sensors, and safety systems. The absence of safety sensors had the side effect of unlocking drift capability that Tesla's software normally manages.

3. **Charging complications:** The car may lack CCS fast-charging enabled in software, leaving Evans with Level 2 charging (7–8 hours) or 110V (14+ hours). Evans has avoided a software update out of concern that it would cause the car to recognize missing components and refuse to operate. After connecting to the Tesla app, the car began displaying "Service Required" messages and became progressively harder to operate — consistent with Tesla's OTA system restricting functionality when critical safety hardware is absent.

## Editorial Stance
Electrek frames the video as an inadvertent demonstration of EV drivetrain durability — arguing that motors and battery packs outlast the rest of the vehicle — and calls it "a compelling advertisement for the longevity of EV powertrains," while noting Tesla would likely prefer a more conventional endorsement.
