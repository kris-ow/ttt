This video presents a financial model arguing that Tesla's robotaxi deployment will become a major stock inflection point, with the creator refining his earlier prediction in response to a community member's challenge to his calculations.

Key Points:

1. The creator originally identified 1,800 fully deployed commercial robotaxis as the inflection point for Tesla's stock, now updated to approximately 2,000 after revising some assumptions.

2. The core revenue assumptions are $1.60–$2.00 per mile in revenue (not the widely cited $1/mile), supported by real-world data showing Tesla currently charges approximately $1.99 per kilometer (~$2.50/mile) in San Francisco, with average fares around $8.17 per ride versus competitors' ~$11.

3. Operating cost (COGS) is assumed at $0.50 per mile, producing a margin of $1.10–$1.50 per mile. Each robotaxi is modeled driving 200 miles/day for 26 days/month.

4. Under these assumptions, a single robotaxi generates approximately $5,720–$7,800 in monthly margin, or roughly $69,000–$93,000 annually — equivalent to the margin from selling 12 Model Y vehicles every year, repeating indefinitely.

5. The original 5% free cash flow benchmark was compared against 2024 Tesla free cash flow of $3.58 billion, not 2025's $6.2 billion — a key clarification correcting the community member's math. At 2,000 robotaxis, the model produces approximately 5.23% of 2024 free cash flow.

6. Scaling projections show dramatic impact: 20,000 robotaxis by December 2026 adds ~50% to Tesla's annual free cash flow; 200,000 robotaxis in 2027 would increase free cash flow by 522%, reaching ~$18.7 billion from robotaxi alone; 500,000 units in 2028 could add ~$50 billion in free cash flow, a ~1,300% increase.

7. Waymo is dismissed as unable to scale and therefore not a real competitor. Uber is identified as the primary threat being displaced, but Uber cannot lower prices ~30% without going bankrupt since it must pay drivers.

8. The inflection point prediction of April–May 2026 was made roughly a year before this video, and the creator notes it remains plausible given current circumstances.

Key Takeaway: The investment thesis rests on the unit economics of robotaxis being dramatically more profitable per asset than car sales, with each vehicle generating the equivalent margin of 12 Model Y sales annually. The critical variables are revenue per mile (likely higher than assumed) and COGS per mile (highly elastic downward with scale). Once 2,000 robotaxis are deployed and generating roughly 5% of 2024 free cash flow on an annualized basis — with that percentage growing rapidly each subsequent month — the creator argues mainstream investors will be forced to reprice Tesla stock significantly higher regardless of current price levels around $400–$410.
