## Key Posts / Themes

Tesla registrations in March surged +330% year-over-year. This is a notable demand inflection in a historically import-competitive market and suggests Tesla's global demand recovery thesis may be gaining traction beyond its core Western markets.

2. **NHTSA Closes "Actually Smart Summon" Investigation — Regulatory Win:** The NHTSA announced it has formally closed its investigation into Tesla's "Actually Smart Summon" feature. The agency found that nearly all reported crashes involved only minor property-damage incidents, with zero injuries and zero fatalities linked to the feature. This is a meaningful regulatory clearing event for Tesla's autonomous and semi-autonomous feature portfolio, reducing one area of legal and reputational overhang and potentially smoothing the path for future autonomous feature approvals.

3. **Terafab: Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI Launch Joint Chip-Building Initiative:** Tesla and SpaceX introduced an updated Terafab website with a new rendering, formally positioning the initiative as a combined effort across Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI to build logic, memory, and advanced packaging under one roof. The stated ambition is to represent "the most epic chip-building effort ever," with the tagline aspiring toward being "a galactic civilization." While grandiose in framing, this is strategically relevant for Tesla investors as in-house chip production could reduce dependence on third-party suppliers (e.g., NVIDIA, Samsung) and support long-term FSD and Optimus compute cost reduction. This post drew 3.1K likes and 392 reposts, indicating meaningful community interest.
