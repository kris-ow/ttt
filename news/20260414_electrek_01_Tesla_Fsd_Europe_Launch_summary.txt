Tesla's FSD launch in Europe is triggering organized legal action from HW3 owners who paid thousands of euros for a product Tesla has now acknowledged it cannot deliver on their hardware. A Dutch owner has launched a collective claim website targeting EU HW3+FSD buyers following the Netherlands' April 10 FSD approval — which applies only to AI4-equipped vehicles.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **Dutch FSD Approval Excludes HW3 Owners:** The Dutch vehicle authority RDW granted Tesla type approval for FSD Supervised under UN R-171 on April 10, 2026 — the first full type approval in the EU and a gateway to broader rollout across the bloc this summer. However, the approved build runs only on Tesla's AI4 computer. Tesla has vaguely promised a "v14 Lite" version for HW3 owners in Q2 2026, but owners and the article characterize it as a materially different product from what was sold.

2. **Tesla's Own Documentation Undermines HW3 Workaround:** Tesla's US patent US20260017503A1, describing the math trick used to run modern FSD on HW3, acknowledges the workaround can render the system "inoperable" for perception units. This compounds prior admissions: VP of AI Ashok Elluswamy stated on August 21, 2024, that HW3 runs a "relatively smaller model" with workarounds, and Elon Musk said on the Q4 2024 earnings call that "we will need to replace all HW3 computers in vehicles where FSD was purchased," calling it "painful and difficult." Roughly 4 million vehicles shipped with HW3; hundreds of thousands of those owners paid for FSD. Fifteen months later, Tesla has no hardware retrofit program, refund policy, or timeline.

3. **Collective Claim Site Launched; Australia Precedent:** Dutch buyer Mischa Sigtermans, who paid €6,400 for FSD in 2019, launched hw3claim.nl to bundle EU HW3+FSD owners for negotiation or legal action. His post drew 75,000 views overnight. In the Netherlands, FSD was priced at €5,300 pre-order and rose to €7,500 post-delivery. A parallel precedent exists in Australia, where thousands of owners joined a class-action in October 2025 led by Echo Law, covering Model 3 and Model Y buyers between May 2021 and February 2025, directly triggered by Musk's HW3 admission.

## Editorial Stance
The author argues Tesla launching FSD in Europe was always going to convert the HW3 problem from abstract to financial liability, that EU consumer protection law is stronger than US or Australian frameworks, and that "v14 Lite" will not satisfy owners who paid for "Full Self-Driving" — predicting the matter will likely end up in court given Tesla's 15-month silence on HW3 remedies.
