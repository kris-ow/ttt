## Robotaxi
1. **Cybercab scaling delay acknowledged:** Jo Bhakdi acknowledged that his earlier prediction of reaching 1,800 unsupervised Cybercab inflection-point vehicles by April or May 2026 is increasingly unlikely to come true. He stated that Cybercab appears to be having scaling issues and that the street's concern — "is robotaxi and Cybercab maybe not scaling?" — is somewhat justified in the near term. He framed this as a delay rather than a failure, but acknowledged it removes a near-term catalyst for Tesla's stock.

2. **FSD version disappointment:** Jo Bhakdi noted that when a newer FSD version (referenced by number as 14.3) was released, Tesla's stock took a small hit because the market was slightly disappointed by it. This contributed to Tesla's relative underperformance around that period.

3. **Long-term robotaxi moat intact:** Despite near-term delays, Jo Bhakdi maintained that there is "no serious competition" for Tesla's robotaxi business in the long run, and similarly "very little competition" on Optimus when considered seriously. He described Tesla as having a hardware-driven moat with no real rival.
