Tesla LEAP options strategy is the central topic of this video, framed around a viewer question from "Dr. Steve Sudell" asking how to structure Tesla LEAP positions after earnings. Key Points:

1. The LEAP roll strategy was invented specifically for Tesla's volatility problem. When an investor is already fully allocated to Tesla and the stock drops 50%, they have no capital left to add. The solution is to sell shares at the depressed price and convert that capital into deep out-of-the-money long-dated calls, which on a capital-adjusted basis tend to outperform the stock when it recovers. For example, selling $100,000 in Tesla stock at $250 and reinvesting into $700 strike calls expiring in 18 months could yield 250-300% if Tesla recovers to $500, versus roughly 100% from just holding shares.

2. Time decay is the central risk in LEAP strategies, and it is far more severe than most investors realize. Using the website optionchart.io, the speaker demonstrates that a Tesla $700 strike call expiring in roughly 90 days costs $169. If Tesla stays flat for just 45 days (half the remaining life), that option drops from $169 to $11, representing a 92% loss on invested capital even though Tesla has not gone down at all. On a $1 million position, that means dropping to roughly $80,000 in 44 days of sideways price action.

3. The practical method for simulating option time decay is to look at a shorter-dated equivalent, not to rely on the Greeks. To estimate what an option will be worth after 45 days of sideways movement, simply look at what the same strike option costs today with 45 fewer days to expiration. The speaker explicitly dismisses delta and other Greeks as misleading because they are non-linear and constantly changing, arguing that empirical simulation is the only reliable method for understanding real-world outcomes.

4. Longer expiration dates dramatically reduce the psychological and financial pain of being early on a thesis. A December 2026 $700 strike call that experiences 6 months of sideways Tesla action will lose approximately 70% of its value (dropping from $22 to around $6). The same scenario with a December 2027 expiration produces only about a 30% loss (dropping from $65 to around $44). On a $1 million position, that's the difference between being left with $300,000 versus $700,000 — a much more survivable position psychologically and financially.

5. The speaker's rule of thumb is a minimum 18-month expiration for any LEAP position. Anything shorter starts entering speculative territory where timing errors become catastrophic. He cites June 17, 2027 calls at a $600 strike as an example of a position he finds interesting, noting that if Tesla reaches $1,000, those calls would return approximately 549% versus roughly 150% from holding shares outright.

6. Currently, the speaker is not holding any LEAPs and is instead using deep in-the-money short puts as a substitute long position. The logic is that in a sideways market, short puts collect time premium rather than losing it. He gives the example of selling a $440 put for $17 when Tesla is at $428 — if Tesla stays flat or rises modestly, the entire premium is collected. The break-even is around $423. The key risk is that short puts carry theoretically unlimited downside: if Tesla drops to $300, a put sold for $17 could be worth $140, creating a catastrophic loss similar to leveraged margin exposure.

Important Conclusions and Recommendations:
Do not use short-dated, deep out-of-the-money LEAPs unless you have very high conviction on precise timing. If uncertain about the exact catalyst date, extend expiration to at least 18 months to survive being wrong by 6 months. Avoid relying on delta or other Greeks for decision-making; use empirical simulation by comparing equivalent shorter-dated options. Short puts are preferable to LEAPs in sideways markets but carry margin-like risk and require constant monitoring. If April passes without the expected inflection, the speaker says he would consider liquidating LEAP positions at a loss and repositioning rather than watching them decay further.
