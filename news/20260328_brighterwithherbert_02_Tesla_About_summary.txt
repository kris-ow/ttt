This video from the "brighterwithherbert" channel covers the rapid nationwide expansion of Tesla's robotaxi preparation activity, arguing that the evidence — spanning job listings, spotted vehicle fleets, and Cybercab validation testing — points to an imminent, simultaneous multi-city rollout rather than a slow sequential one. Host David is joined by guest Larry Goldberg, a serial entrepreneur and venture capital investor, who shares his assessment of the rollout's progress, infrastructure readiness, and remaining risks. The core thesis is that Tesla is moving at "warp speed" compared to competitors and is on track to cover a significant portion of the US population within months.

## Robotaxi

1. **Nationwide AI Safety Operator Hiring Surge:** Tesla is now actively hiring AI safety operators in 18 cities across 12 states, up from a previously reported 14 cities across 9 states. Four new cities were added just days before the video was published, including Washington DC, San Diego, Brooklyn Park, and Aurora, Colorado. An analysis by "Cern Basher" determined that these 18 cities collectively cover nearly 40% of the US population, and identified additional cities (shown in green on a map) that, if added, would bring coverage to approximately 50% of the US population.

2. **Simultaneous Multi-City Rollout Strategy:** Rather than a sequential city-by-city launch, the hosts argue Tesla appears to be staging a near-simultaneous nationwide rollout. Larry Goldberg speculates there are likely more staging lots and fleets in cities that haven't yet been spotted, and suggests Tesla could be in 50% of US states within a couple of months. The pattern of activity — hiring, fleet staging, and validation testing happening in parallel across many cities — is interpreted as a sign of Tesla's urgency and rollout speed.

3. **Rollout Model: Supervised First, Then Driverless:** Goldberg explains that for the next 10–20 cities Tesla enters, each will initially deploy vehicles with a safety driver in the car. Once confidence is established with local regulators and customers, the safety driver will be withdrawn, transitioning to a remote supervisor model. He draws a parallel to Austin, where this transition is already underway. He notes that in his personal experience riding the Austin robotaxi both with and without a safety driver, there was functionally no difference.

4. **Dallas Robotaxi "Hive" Spotted:** A user named Chris Durov discovered what appears to be a large Dallas robotaxi staging lot — a "sea" of Tesla Model Y vehicles all equipped with rear camera washers and Texas license plates. These vehicles have been observed driving on roads and simulating pickup and drop-off behavior. The rear camera washer is described as a key identifier, as it is not available on consumer-purchased Model Y vehicles.

5. **Fleets Spotted Across Multiple Cities:** Beyond Dallas, Model Y vehicles with rear camera washers and Texas plates have been spotted in Phoenix, Arizona and Orlando, Florida. Two validation Model Y vehicles were spotted in Houston. A large lot of Teslas was also identified in the suburbs of Las Vegas. The hosts note this is only what has been publicly spotted and suggest the actual number of staging locations is likely much higher.

6. **Cybercab Spotted in San Francisco with Validation Rig:** For the first time, a Cybercab equipped with a validation rig and notably lacking both a steering wheel and side mirrors was spotted in San Francisco. This is described as a significant milestone, as previous Cybercab validation rig sightings had been confined to the Austin/Giga Texas area. Goldberg suggests California could remove the safety driver requirement within months, potentially weeks, opening the door to a true driverless Cybercab deployment there.

7. **Model Y as Bridge Fleet Before Cybercab Scale:** Goldberg explains the strategic logic behind deploying fleets of Model Y vehicles now: Cybercab production in April will start at very low volumes — estimated at a few thousand units initially — and mass production is described as more than five months away. Tesla does not want to wait, so Model Y robotaxis are being deployed immediately to build out operations in new cities. By November or December 2026, Goldberg projects a flood of robotaxis, making current fleet sizes look like "a drop in the bucket." He also notes that unlike Waymo, Tesla can eventually scale to producing thousands of Cybercabs per day.

8. **Cybercab vs. Model Y Transition Expected to Be Smooth:** On the question of what it will take to add the Cybercab to the live fleet alongside Model Y, Goldberg argues the transition will be very smooth. He notes that the Model Y robotaxis in Austin are already operating fully autonomously — the safety driver is purely supervisory and makes no interventions. The FSD system is doing all the driving, so transitioning to the steeringwheel-free Cybercab is not a fundamentally different operational challenge.

9. **Infrastructure Readiness:** Goldberg addresses infrastructure as a key consideration, noting that Tesla service centers are already being "primed" to support robotaxi fleets. He gives the example of Raleigh, North Carolina, where Tesla expanded from one location to three within the same city, and also added a body shop. He describes the AI safety operators currently in the field as already stress-testing the operational logistics — charging, cleaning, parking, routing — so that by the time full commercial service launches, the systems are proven.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Version 14.2.2.5 Showing Contextual Reasoning:** The video discusses a clip of a Cybertruck running FSD version 14.2.2.5 that demonstrated what appears to be contextual reasoning — the vehicle backed up to yield space when a semi-truck needed to pass, understanding the broader traffic context. Goldberg says he observed similar behavior on an earlier version (14.2.2.2), suggesting this capability may not be new to the .5 release but was already present in .2. The hosts interpret this as evidence that reasoning capabilities are gradually being integrated into FSD.

2. **FSD Version 14.3 as a Key Upcoming Milestone:** The hosts discuss FSD 14.3 as a critical upcoming release, describing it as what Elon Musk has called the "final piece" needed before broader unsupervised deployment. The hypothesis presented is that Tesla deliberately slowed the expansion of unsupervised robotaxi operations while waiting for 14.3 (with more reasoning capabilities) to be ready. Goldberg humorously corrects that the actual release will likely be 14.3.2 or 14.3.3 before it is truly considered stable. David frames the release of 14.3 as the likely trigger for a major acceleration in the robotaxi rollout.

3. **FSD Performance Across Diverse Geographies:** Both hosts note that FSD already works effectively in a wide range of environments. The host mentions personally running FSD in Vancouver, Canada and Seattle without issues. Goldberg describes FSD performing well in the narrow, winding mountain roads of North Carolina with four-way stops and unusual turns, suggesting the system's generalizability means city-by-city geographic readiness is less of a constraint than it might appear.

## Electric Vehicles

Production is expected to begin in April 2026, described as being literally days away at the time of recording. Goldberg compares the ramp-up pattern to the Model Y launch — early drips followed by a production surge — but predicts Cybercab will ultimately ramp faster than Model Y did.
