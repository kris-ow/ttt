## Executive Summary
Today's posts center on three high-signal themes for Tesla investors: the rapid expansion of Tesla's unsupervised robotaxi fleet in Austin (now confirmed at 13 vehicles), new international patents pointing to an Optimus V3 hand design, and Samsung's commencement of equipment installation at its Taylor, Texas chip facility for Tesla. The overall tone is bullish, with concrete, observable progress across multiple pillars of Tesla's autonomous and AI thesis.

## Key Posts / Themes

1. **Unsupervised Robotaxi Fleet Expanding in Austin:** In a span of hours on April 16, Sawyer updated the Austin unsupervised Model Y robotaxi count twice — first to 12 vehicles, then to 13. These are unsupervised (driverless) Model Ys being spotted operating in Austin, suggesting Tesla is actively scaling its pre-commercial robotaxi fleet ahead of a broader launch. Sawyer also commented that based on the volume of testing observed this week, a previously circulated timeline for robotaxi deployment "has a higher chance of actually coming to fruition," though no specific launch date was named. Separately, Sawyer dismissed skepticism about Tesla's AI4 hardware achieving unsupervised capability, stating it is "literally already doing it."

2. **Optimus V3 Hand Patents Published:** Tesla published multiple international patents today describing what appears to be the hand and forearm design for Optimus V3. Key disclosed specifications include: actuators located in the forearm (not the hand itself), each finger featuring 4 degrees of freedom, the wrist featuring 2 degrees of freedom, and a tendon/cable-driven actuation system. Sawyer references a prior statement from Elon Musk (made last November) that Optimus would have 25 actuators in each forearm and hand. The posts note anticipation for a full V3 Optimus unveil, though no reveal date is specified. This patent publication is the highest-engagement post of the day (3.5K likes, 397 retweets), reflecting strong investor interest.

3. **Samsung Texas Chip Facility Begins Equipment Installation for Tesla:** Samsung Electronics has completed preparations at its Taylor, Texas foundry and will hold a major equipment installation ceremony on April 24. The plant will manufacture chips for Tesla (specific chip details were cut off in the post). This marks a meaningful milestone in Tesla's chip supply chain, with implications for FSD and Optimus compute capacity.

4. **Model S and Model X Signature Editions Sell Out:** The $160,000 Model S Signature Edition sold out (reservations closed), following the earlier sellout of the $160,000 Model X Signature Edition. Across the 250 Model S and 100 Model X Signature Edition units, Tesla generated a combined $56 million in revenue. While limited in scale, this signals resilient demand at the high end of Tesla's lineup.

5. **Tesla Factory Innovation — Giga Casting Cycle Time Improvement:** Tesla released a video featuring Lars discussing manufacturing innovations originating at Giga Berlin. A notable data point: Giga casting cycle time has been reduced to 68 seconds, down from 120 seconds when Tesla began at Fremont in 2020 — a roughly 43% improvement. Additional innovations mentioned include an automatic line for hanging fenders, hoods, etc. This is relevant to Tesla's cost reduction thesis and Cybercab production readiness.

6. **Market & Competition — Mercedes All-Electric C-Class Interior Reveal:** Mercedes unveiled the interior of its new all-electric C-Class, featuring a pillar-to-pillar display, 3D/4D soundscapes, faster cabin heating than combustion models, a heat pump using ~50% less energy, and smart humidity control. Included here as a competitive data point; the EV competitive landscape continues to develop at the premium segment.
