Jo Bhakdi analyzes the broader market rally on what he describes as the best NASDAQ day of the year (up 3.24%), driven by signals that the Iran conflict may be de-escalating and Fed commentary suggesting potential rate cuts. The core thesis is that Tesla and Nvidia are "coiled springs" set up for a significant rally, with Tesla specifically positioned for a major Q2 2026 inflection point tied to FSD v14.3 and Cybercab production ramping. However, he cautions that a 25% tail risk of further Iran escalation justifies remaining underexposed and avoiding leverage.

## Autonomous Driving
1. **FSD v14.3 as the "Final Unlock" of Full Autonomy:** Jo Bhakdi states that FSD version 14.3 represents what he believes will be the final unlock of full autonomy, and he expects it to arrive within the next 14 days at minimum, or within the next month. He describes this as a critical catalyst for Tesla's stock repricing, arguing that Tesla has already prepared multiple cities for autonomous deployment and that the combination of this software release with Cybercab production ramping could mark a genuine, large-scale rollout of autonomy.

2. **Q2 2026 as the Autonomy Inflection Point:** Bhakdi assigns a 75% or higher probability that Q2 2026 is the moment Tesla rolls out autonomy "for real." He frames this as a potential "absolute gamechanger" that could initiate what he calls "the great repricing of the stock," contrasting it with Q1 2026 which he says played out exactly as he predicted — a period of deflation with no catalysts.

## Robotaxi
1. **Cybercab Production Ramp as a Key Catalyst:** Bhakdi highlights that Cybercab production is ramping up and, combined with FSD v14.3 and pre-prepared city deployments, could represent the convergence of catalysts needed to trigger Tesla's revaluation. He refers to this combination as a potentially historic moment for the company.

## Market & Competition
1. **NASDAQ Year-to-Date Performance and Market Context:** The NASDAQ is down 7.64% year-to-date as of the video, making the single-day 3.24% gain the best day of the year. Bhakdi characterizes the prior months as an "extremely exhausting grind" and a "pretty much a bloodbath," driven by Iran conflict uncertainty, oil price disruption via the Strait of Hormuz blockade, and broader macro fears.

2. **Nvidia's Valuation as a Market Signal:** Bhakdi notes that Nvidia currently has its lowest price-to-earnings ratio in 7 years, despite being up 4.66% on the day. He states Nvidia has dropped below a $4 trillion market cap and is also down roughly 7% year-to-date. He argues this extreme compression in Nvidia's valuation, alongside dramatic margin growth and AI positioning, illustrates how much of a "coiled spring" the broader AI market represents. He predicts Nvidia will rally back to $190 "at the millisecond" the Iran situation resolves.

3. **Tesla Down 18% Year-to-Date:** Tesla is down approximately 18% year-to-date, underperforming even Bhakdi's own Pioneer Alpha portfolio (which he says is down roughly 10-11%). He frames this underperformance as expected given the lack of catalysts in Q1, and argues it makes the Q2 setup even more compelling.

4. **Geopolitical Tail Risk Framing:** Bhakdi estimates a 75% probability the market has bottomed and a 25% tail risk of further escalation in Iran. He warns that if the U.S. deploys boots on the ground, it would be a "complete disaster," potentially sending oil to $150–$200 and devastating markets through at least 2027. He views this low-probability but high-impact scenario as the primary reason to avoid leverage despite the attractive setup.
