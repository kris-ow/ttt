This video is a conversational discussion between host Brian and co-host Randy on the Futurazza channel, covering a range of Tesla-related news including a potential FSD version release, the end of Model S and Model X production, a legal victory for Elon Musk following judicial bias allegations, Cybercab production updates, and Q1 2026 delivery estimates. ## Autonomous Driving
1. **FSD Version 14.3 Reportedly Rolling Out to Employees:** Multiple sources reported that FSD version 14.3 is being distributed to Tesla employees, with speculation it could reach broader customers as soon as the weekend of the video's publication (around April 4–5, 2026). The hosts noted that an initial announcement from a user named Omar included features that seemed implausibly advanced, leading them to suspect an April Fools' joke. However, a subsequent, more measured post attributed to Elon Musk confirmed the employee rollout and described improvements, leading the hosts to take the release more seriously given Musk's reputation for not joking on such matters.

2. **Core Bull Thesis Tied to Autonomy:** Randy reiterated the channel's long-standing investment thesis, first articulated around January 2023: if you believe unsupervised robotaxis will operate at scale globally, you should own Tesla stock. If you don't believe that, you shouldn't. The hosts expressed frustration at social media commentators who argue the autonomous future will never materialize, suggesting those skeptics simply wait for cancellations rather than investing based on that view.

3. **FSD Adoption Rate Among Tesla Owners:** The hosts noted that FSD adoption among Tesla owners in the US is only around 20%, which Brian described as "crazy" given the significance of the technology. This was cited in the context of comparing Tesla to competitors like Rivian and Lucid, which lack any FSD equivalent.

## Robotaxi
1. **Cybercab Production Confirmed at Austin Factory:** The hosts cited a news article stating that Tesla has begun producing the Cybercab — described as a fully autonomous, steering-wheel-free vehicle designed for ride-hailing — at its Austin factory. The hosts framed this as a major strategic pivot toward AI-driven mobility and a direct challenge to Uber and Lyft, though they acknowledged that regulatory approval and public trust remain hurdles to widespread deployment.

2. **Public Trust as a Robotaxi Hurdle:** Randy discussed the challenge of public acceptance of autonomous vehicles, using Elon Musk's elevator analogy — that early elevator operators were eventually replaced by automation, and some people were initially fearful. Randy predicted that even five to ten years from now, a segment of the population will refuse to ride in autonomous vehicles, though he views this as a temporary adoption curve rather than a structural barrier.

## Electric Vehicles
1. **End of Model S and Model X Production:** Tesla sent an email announcing the end of production for the Model S and Model X, which both hosts acknowledged as emotionally significant but strategically rational. Randy argued that continuing to invest in incremental upgrades to low-volume vehicles — potentially only 20,000–50,000 units per year — would not justify the cost, and that Tesla's future lies in rethinking vehicle design from scratch, as demonstrated by the Cybercab and Cybertruck. Brian agreed, framing the Model 3 and Model Y as transitional "bridge" vehicles rather than the end destination.

2. **Tesla vs. Competitors on Value:** The hosts discussed Lucid and Rivian as strong vehicles in terms of raw capability — particularly Rivian's R1S — but emphasized that without FSD, they are simply cars competing on range and quality metrics. The hosts noted that Lucid offers 500 miles of range but at a price premium that limits its appeal, reinforcing Tesla's value proposition when FSD is factored in.

## Market & Competition
1. **European Sales Surging for Tesla in Early 2026:** The hosts reported that Tesla sales data emerging from Europe showed significant gains across multiple countries including France, the Netherlands, Sweden, Portugal, and Spain, with some markets up 100–200% year-over-year. In contrast, BYD was reported down in the same period. The hosts cited this data as contradicting analyst predictions of a bad Q1 for Tesla.

2. **EV Adoption S-Curve Across Regions:** Randy described global EV adoption as following a classic S-curve disruption pattern, with different countries at different stages. Norway and Northern European countries are climbing the curve rapidly, China is approaching 50% EV and hybrid penetration, and California is in the 20% range. The hosts suggested rising gasoline prices (described as a "gas shock") could accelerate EV adoption, particularly in Europe.

3. **Analyst and Media Skepticism Criticized:** The hosts mocked a Motley Fool article warning that Tesla's stock remains "priced for perfection" and highlighting shrinking margins, noting the stock was up more than 10% since the article was published the prior day (from approximately $356 to $381). They framed this as a recurring pattern of analysts failing to account for Tesla's autonomous driving potential.
