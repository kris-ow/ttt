## Key Posts / Themes

1. **Model S/X Signature Edition — Final Production Run Details:** Merritt spoke directly with customers who were offered the Model S and Model X Signature Editions, framing these as "the final versions of the Model S/X that will ever be produced." The Model X Signature Edition is priced at $159K (post appears truncated, so the full price breakdown and Model S pricing were not fully captured). A resale restriction of one year post-delivery applies to both models. Notably, Tesla Senior VP of Automotive Tom Zhu personally ordered a Model X Signature Edition, signaling internal confidence in the product. Merritt noted the resale restriction is unlikely to be an issue for the target buyer profile. This is a meaningful EV signal — Tesla is formally end-of-lifing the S/X platform with a collector's edition, which could compress future demand for those lines but also extracts margin from enthusiast buyers.

2. **FSD (Supervised) Launches in Europe — Netherlands Goes Live:** Tesla owners in the Netherlands are now actively using FSD (Supervised) on their own vehicles, marking a long-awaited regulatory approval milestone for Europe. Merritt called it "pretty surreal" given how many years the approval process took, and noted that FSD handles the complexities of European roads "very well." A new FSD start page UI specific to the Netherlands rollout was also highlighted, featuring a daily streaks feature not currently available in North America. This is a meaningful geographic expansion of FSD, opening a new market for FSD subscriptions or package revenue.

3. **Tesla Megapack — $80M Order in Belgium:** Tesla secured an $80 million Megapack contract with Belgian green energy firm Energy Solutions Group for a 76 MW / 304 MWh battery energy storage system located in Sambreville, Belgium. The system is expected to connect to the grid by 2027. This adds to Tesla's visible Energy backlog and confirms continued international demand for utility-scale storage.

4. **Tesla Semi at ACT Expo — May 2026:** Tesla will bring the production version of the Tesla Semi to the ACT Expo in Las Vegas (May 4–7, 2026), the largest North American advanced commercial transportation trade show. CDL holders will be able to test drive it; others can participate in ride-alongs. This is a product visibility and commercial demand-generation event, though no new production or delivery figures were announced.
