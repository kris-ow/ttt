This video from "brighterwithherbert" features host Bert and guest Jeff Lutz — a multi-decade supply chain executive and CEO of a high-tech manufacturing consulting firm — reviewing a wave of institutional analyst upgrades and reiterations on Tesla, centered on the thesis that autonomous driving is approaching a major inflection point. The discussion revisits Elon Musk's Q2 2025 earnings call prediction that Tesla's economics would become "very compelling" once autonomy reaches scale in the second half of 2026, and evaluates whether that timeline is still on track. The video also covers Tesla's Model Y dominance in China, FSD's first European regulatory approval, the significance of the 10 billion cumulative FSD miles milestone, and a detailed update on EOS Energy, a long-duration battery storage company.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **First FSD Supervised Approval in Europe:** The Dutch Vehicle Authority granted Tesla approval to operate Full Self-Driving supervised in the Netherlands following more than 18 months of testing and mapping analysis. The hosts note this was not a robotaxi (unsupervised) approval but rather a supervised FSD approval, which they view as a meaningful regulatory milestone. They suggest other European Union countries could potentially adopt it relatively quickly, possibly by end of 2026.

2. **10 Billion Cumulative FSD Miles Milestone:** Morgan Stanley cited a public tracker showing Tesla owners are driving 18.5 million miles with FSD supervised every day, putting the company on pace to surpass 10 billion cumulative FSD miles by late May 2026. The hosts clarify that Elon Musk's framing of this milestone was never a precise threshold — when asked at a shareholder meeting, Musk described it as an order-of-magnitude estimate ("more than 1 billion and probably less than 100 billion"). The significance is that accumulated miles provide training data for edge cases to statistically prove unsupervised reliability, particularly to regulators. The hosts note Tesla is described as already being nine times safer, and speculate that FSD could reduce the U.S. annual auto fatality count from approximately 43,000 to around 4,000.

3. **Tesla's Vision-Only Moat:** Bank of America analyst Alexander Perry reiterated a buy rating with a $460 price target, calling Tesla the clear leader in autonomous driving because its camera-only approach is more cost-effective and scalable. Perry stated Tesla's strategy should allow it to scale more profitably than robotaxi competitors. Jeff Lutz emphasizes the moat is large and growing, and that competitors receiving Nvidia development kits should not be confused with having a finished autonomous system — they would still need teams with the AI expertise to replicate years of Tesla's training work, without the billions of real-world miles of data.

## Robotaxi

1. **Elon's Autonomy-at-Scale Timeline and Current Status:** Revisiting the Q2 2025 earnings call clip, Elon predicted Tesla could have "a few rough quarters" in Q4 2025, Q1 and possibly Q2 2026, but that by the second half of 2026 Tesla's economics would become compelling once autonomy reaches scale. He added his guess was a "material impact on financials around the end of next year" (i.e., end of 2026). The hosts and Jeff Lutz agree Tesla is behind in the first 90 days of 2026 but argue that because the new revenue model is software-driven rather than hardware-limited, Tesla has the ability to catch up. They acknowledge meaningful financial impact may now slip into the first half of 2027.

2. **Supervised vs. Unsupervised Rollout Path:** Jeff Lutz explains that Tesla's initial robotaxi deployment in new cities will likely involve supervised operation — essentially FSD supervised carrying passengers — which is more operationally gated by Tesla itself rather than regulators or hardware constraints. Scaling into the hundreds and thousands of vehicles per city, however, will require unsupervised regulatory approval. He frames the key unanswered question for the upcoming earnings call as: were delays operational, software-related, or tied to regulatory submission windows on a Gantt chart?

3. **Hardware Scalability Advantage:** Jeff Lutz argues Tesla is not hardware-limited unlike competitors. Tesla can produce Model Y robotaxis at approximately 5,000 units per day or more if needed, and the Cybercab is described as "largely on track" from announcements made in the second half of 2025. This positions Tesla to scale rapidly once software and regulatory conditions are met.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Model Y Number One in China Despite Premium Pricing:** Tesla's Model Y was the number one selling vehicle in China in March 2026 across all vehicle types — including EVs, gas, and hybrids. Tesla's Model Y sells at approximately $38,500 USD equivalent, while BYD and Geely offer competing vehicles at approximately $10,000–$13,000 USD equivalent — roughly three times cheaper. Jeff Lutz argues this exposes a deep profitability problem for Chinese EV manufacturers who are selling high volumes at low price points and are unlikely to be profitable on those models, while Tesla maintains strong margins at scale.

2. **German Registrations Recovering:** Cantor Fitzgerald's note briefly mentioned that German registrations had appeared delayed but have since turned around for Tesla, suggesting recovery in the European market.

## Financials

1. **Analyst Price Targets and Ratings:** Three major institutional analysts reiterated or maintained bullish stances on Tesla around mid-April 2026. Cantor Fitzgerald reiterated an overweight rating with a $510 price target, specifically citing European FSD regulatory developments. Morgan Stanley maintained a $415 price target with a bull case of $845 and a bear case of $135, assigning roughly 24% probability to the base case price being reached by April 2027, a 6% chance of exceeding $845, and a 6% chance of falling below $135. Bank of America maintained a buy rating with a $460 price target. The current stock price at time of recording was approximately $345–$349.

2. **Revenue Model Transition:** Morgan Stanley's note framed the current period as one where Tesla is transitioning from a predominantly hardware revenue model (with 15–20% gross margins) to a software-driven model. Jeff Lutz argues that once the software percentage of revenue begins ramping visibly, the stock narrative will shift — potentially driving margins into the 50–70% range. He also references Tesla's vertical integration into silicon as a medium-term development that could eventually be monetized by selling to other companies.

3. **Capex and Free Cash Flow Warning:** Morgan Stanley noted that Tesla's capex is set to more than double in 2026 and free cash flow is expected to flip negative. They described Tesla's ability to demonstrate tangible progress in scaling unsupervised autonomous driving as critical to supporting valuation in this environment.

4. **Upcoming Q1 2026 Earnings:** The hosts note Tesla's Q1 2026 earnings will be reported approximately 9 days from the recording date, making it a near-term catalyst to watch.

## Market & Competition

1. **China EV Profitability Problem:** Jeff Lutz argues that Chinese EV manufacturers producing vehicles at $10,000–$15,000 price points should theoretically be outselling the Model Y by five to ten times but are not. He draws a parallel to Apple's situation if the iPhone SE at $399 outsold only a fraction of premium-tier iPhones, suggesting the low-price strategy is not translating into the volume needed to justify the economics. He singles out Xiaomi as the one Chinese EV company that is keeping its model count low and staying highly automated — similar to Tesla's strategy — and notes it is also not struggling in the same way.

2. **Competitors Unlikely to Scale:** Jeff Lutz expresses skepticism about most robotaxi and humanoid robot competitors achieving scale, citing a pattern from his supply chain career of projects that appear technically sound but collapse under poor unit economics. He references Uber employees now being able to hail a robotaxi using a Neuro vehicle (described as a Lucid Gravity-based platform) via their employee app but characterizes this as early-stage demonstration rather than scalable deployment. He draws the same conclusion about the majority of humanoid robots showcased at industry events, suggesting 90% will not reach scale.

## Energy

1. **Tesla Energy Product Expansion:** The Cantor Fitzgerald note cited Tesla's Semi and Megapack as remaining on track for volume production in 2026. The hosts also reference a Megapack 3 and a product described as the "Mega Block," characterized as a long-duration storage product aimed at a new total addressable market — specifically data centers and city-level installations seeking long-duration storage. This is described as an expansion of Tesla Energy's product portfolio beyond existing capacity.

2. **EOS Energy Long-Duration Battery Storage Update:** EOS Energy, a zinc-halide (aqueous, non-flammable) long-duration battery storage company, had a difficult Q4 2025 earnings report — revenue came in in the "upper 50s" (millions) versus projected $90–$100 million, after the company had reiterated guidance with 7 weeks remaining in the quarter. Output reached only 2–2.5x prior quarter volume when 3x was needed. However, in recent weeks EOS pre-announced that Q1 2026 is on track within guided range, a second manufacturing line has passed factory acceptance testing at its supplier and is being installed (more than doubling capacity), and a new 400,000+ square foot facility lease has been signed to improve line layout efficiency and throughput. EOS also launched a new higher-density system configuration in January 2026 delivering approximately 4x the energy density per acre of a typical Megapack system, and introduced Dawn OS, an AI management software layer that allows the system to compensate for individual cell failures without degrading overall performance. The company has booked $240 million in orders, running at approximately a $1 billion annualized order rate. Large potential orders include one from New York City and one from a UK cap-and-floor program. EOS's product is described as 91% U.S.-sourced, heading to 96% once a component currently coming from Mexico is localized. Formal Q1 2026 earnings are expected in May 2026.
