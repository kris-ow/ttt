## Key Posts / Themes

1. **Cybercabs Spotted at Giga Texas — Production Ramp Underway:** The most significant post of the day (4.2K likes, 355 RTs) showed 14 Cybercabs without steering wheels spotted at Giga Texas. This is a meaningful visual confirmation that Tesla is producing Cybercabs at scale in a configuration aligned with fully driverless, commercial robotaxi deployment. The absence of steering wheels is the critical detail — it indicates these units are not development mules but production-intent vehicles designed for unsupervised autonomous operation. This is the strongest near-term production signal for the robotaxi thesis seen in recent weeks.

2. **Tesla 2026 Spring Update — New Self-Driving App Rolls Out:** Tesla began rolling out its 2026 Spring software update, which includes a new dedicated Self-Driving app allowing users to subscribe with a single tap, learn how to activate and use the feature, and view ongoing stats. This represents a meaningful UX step toward broader FSD commercialization and easier consumer onboarding. Additional updates include an Immersive Sound Upgrade for Premium audio. The Self-Driving app infrastructure also hints at the subscription and fleet management architecture that will underpin the robotaxi network.

3. **Tesla Semi — Detailed Production Trim Specs Released:** Tesla released new visuals and an updated website for the production Tesla Semi, revealing two trims. The Standard Range version has approximately 325 miles of range, a turning radius comparable to a Model Y, uses 4680 cells designed to last 1 million miles, and features fully electric steering assist (replacing the previous hydraulic system). Tesla's updated website emphasizes lower per-mile electricity costs versus diesel and significantly reduced maintenance costs due to the absence of diesel aftertreatment systems. These are the most detailed production specs released to date and suggest Tesla is actively building commercial momentum for the Semi.

4. **Q1 Earnings Setup — Wall Street Consensus and Investor Questions:** Tesla released its company-compiled analyst consensus for Q1 2026 earnings (call scheduled for next week): Revenue of $21.4B, GAAP EPS of $0.16, Gross Margin of 17.5%, and Free Cash Flow of -$1.57B. The top investor-voted questions for the call include: (1) When will the Optimus v3 be revealed and when will Optimus production start, given that Model X and S production ended earlier than midyear; and (2) what is the expected Optimus production ramp. These questions signal that investors will be closely scrutinizing the humanoid robot timeline as a primary valuation driver on the call.

5. **Terafab Chip Project — Taiwan Hiring for Sub-7nm Processes:** Tesla posted nine engineering roles in Taiwan for its "Terafab" project, requiring candidates with more than five years of experience in advanced chipmaking processes below 7 nanometers. This is a notable development for Tesla's vertical integration strategy in AI/autonomous driving silicon, and aligns with the watch item around Samsung/Tesla chip production milestones. The sub-7nm node requirement suggests Tesla is targeting cutting-edge process technology for its next-generation chips.

6. **EV Market Expansion — Estonia Launch and India Model Y L:** Tesla announced an official launch event in Estonia on April 24th at Ülemiste Center, featuring test drives and a first showcase of the Cybertruck in the country. Separately, Tesla is reportedly set to launch the Model Y L in India as soon as next week. Both moves reflect continued geographic expansion, though these are lower-signal items for the core autonomous/robotaxi thesis.

7. **Tesla Sues North Dakota Over Direct Sales Ban:** Tesla filed a lawsuit against North Dakota after the state refused to allow Tesla to open showrooms or service centers and sell directly to consumers, citing a state law requiring sales through franchised dealerships. This has moderate relevance to Tesla's ability to scale service infrastructure in the U.S., which is an indirect dependency for robotaxi fleet servicing.
