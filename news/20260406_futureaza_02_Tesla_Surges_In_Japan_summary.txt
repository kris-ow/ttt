This Monday news roundup from Futuraza (Brian) covers approximately 20 stories related to Tesla and the broader EV industry as of April 6, 2026. The video's core thesis is that Tesla continues to make meaningful market gains globally — particularly in Japan and South Korea — while broader EV adoption is accelerating due to rising fuel costs, and that Tesla's inventory build and Model Y L expansion position it well for near-term demand capture.

## Electric Vehicles

1. **Tesla Tops Korea's Imported Car Market for First Time:** Tesla ranked number one in Korea's imported car market in Q1 2026, marking the first time it has taken the top quarterly spot. Tesla outsold BMW (19,000 units) and Mercedes (15,000 units) by roughly 25–30%, with Tesla registering approximately 20,000+ units. Brian highlights this as particularly significant given Korea's strong loyalty to domestic automotive brands.

2. **Tesla Eyes Japan's Top Imported Car Spot:** Tesla is planning to expand to at least 60 stores in Japan as it seeks to become the country's biggest imported car brand by as early as 2027. The article cited in the video notes Tesla has already sold half as many units in Q1 2026 as it did in all of 2025, indicating strong momentum. Japan's lack of domestic oil production makes EV running costs increasingly attractive.

3. **Model S/X Inventory Nearly Exhausted — Price Raised $15,000:** Tesla has raised the price on all remaining new and demo Model S/X vehicles in inventory by $15,000, rather than discounting to clear stock. In Texas, inventory dropped from three or four units to just two over a single day. Brian frames this as rational behavior — Tesla has no urgency to move the final units, and these represent the last new Model S/X units that will ever be sold.

4. **Tesla Records 50,000-Vehicle Q1 Surplus — Brian Dismisses Concerns:** A headline flagged a 50,000-vehicle new inventory surplus in Q1 2026, framed as a demand concern. Brian counters that this represents only about 11 days of inventory, and that having vehicles on hand is strategically important to capture impulse buyers and customers whose vehicles break down unexpectedly — especially as gas prices rise. He expects the inventory to be drawn down during Q2 2026.

5. **Model Y L Expands to Eight-Plus New Markets:** The longer-wheelbase Model Y L has expanded to additional Asia-Pacific markets including Japan, South Korea, Hong Kong, Macau, Singapore, Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines, Australia, New Zealand, and China. Brian describes the product as generating significant consumer enthusiasm, with lines forming around dealerships.

6. **EV Demand Surging in Australia and New Zealand:** Rising fuel costs in Australia are driving up used EV prices — Model 3 and Y have risen 5–6%, while petrol vehicles have seen the largest price cuts. In New Zealand, a single BYD salesman sold 87 EVs in one month versus a typical 20, and BYD nationally is now moving 200–300 units per weekend versus a prior 300 per month. Used BYD Atto 3 resale values have risen NZD 3,000–5,000 in just three weeks.

7. **Japan Cuts BYD Subsidies, Tesla Retains Full Subsidy:** Japan introduced new EV subsidy rules favoring locally sourced batteries. BYD and apparently other brands (such as the Nissan Ariya) saw subsidy cuts for not meeting domestic parts thresholds. Tesla reportedly retained the full subsidy — reportedly increased to ¥790,000 — though Brian expresses uncertainty about the reasoning, noting Tesla's current vehicles use Chinese batteries rather than Panasonic cells.

8. **Global EV Sales Scorecard — February:** The worldwide EV sales ranking for February placed Model Y first and Model 3 second, followed by Geely and BYD models. Brian notes Tesla's continued top ranking with just two core models as an example of efficiency through product focus.

9. **Subaru Getaway EV Unveiled:** The Subaru Getaway, a three-row SUV comparable to the Toyota Highlander, offers up to 300 miles of range on a 97–98 kWh battery pack. It features a 221 hp single-motor front-wheel-drive entry level up to 338 hp, dual wireless charging pads, and optional leather upholstery. Brian notes the 300-mile figure assumes 0–100% charge cycling, which is unrealistic in practice.

## Market & Competition

1. **BYD Launches 5,000 Megawatt Flash Charging Stations:** BYD has opened 5,000 second-generation flash charging stations, capable of charging at up to 1.5 megawatts — faster than a Tesla Semi. Brian accepts BYD's confidence in battery safety given their scale as a battery manufacturer, and frames this as BYD's strategy to recover demand momentum.

2. **BYD Denza GT to Star in Next James Bond Film:** BYD's Denza GT, one of the vehicles compatible with its flash charging network, has been selected to appear in the next James Bond movie — an unusual but notable mainstream cultural moment for a Chinese EV brand.

3. **BYD Launches First Overseas Sky Rail in São Paulo:** BYD has begun operations on a Sky Rail transit route in São Paulo, Brazil, marking its first overseas rail transit deployment. Brian expresses surprise at BYD's breadth of operations spanning EVs, batteries, and now transit infrastructure.

4. **China Eliminates Full EV Tax Exemption:** China has removed the full EV purchase tax exemption; buyers now face a 5% purchase tax, though the benefit is capped. Brian notes this affected both Tesla and BYD's Q1 numbers and reflects a natural policy progression as EV adoption matures.

5. **Tesla Regains Global EV Lead Over BYD:** Tesla sold approximately 358,000 EVs globally, edging out BYD for the global EV lead. Brian acknowledges that BYD's underperformance is a mixed signal — good for Tesla's relative ranking but indicative of broader demand softness.

6. **Solid-State Motorcycle Claim Debunked:** Brian calls out Verge Motorcycles (owned by the same entity as battery company Donut) for falsely claiming deliveries had started in Q1 2026. The company shipped one unit to an undisclosed recipient and refuses to identify the buyer, which Brian argues is not a credible delivery milestone. He positions this as a cautionary example of EV hype versus honest reporting.

## Energy

1. **Megapack Factory Visit Planned:** Brian mentions he will be visiting the Megapack factory in Lethbridge in a couple of weeks and is preparing questions. He notes that Megapack deployments are constrained not by manufacturing speed but by permitting, inspections, and grid certification — factors outside Tesla's direct control. He references "Mega Block" as Tesla's effort to pre-integrate components to reduce on-site approval steps.

2. **Solar Saves Europe €3 Billion in March:** Solar generation saved European consumers approximately €3 billion in fossil fuel imports in March 2026 alone, though Brian notes the pace of solar growth is slower than he'd like to see.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **NHTSA Closes Smart Summon Investigation — Zero Injuries:** NHTSA closed its investigation into Tesla's Smart Summon feature, finding that nearly all reported crashes involved minor property damage with zero injuries and zero fatalities linked to the feature. Out of millions of summon sessions, fewer than 1% resulted in any incident. Brian notes this is still not a fully clean result for an unsupervised scenario but is far better than critics claimed.

2. **Current Model Y's Ability to Run Unsupervised FSD Questioned:** Brian expresses skepticism that the current Model Y can perform unsupervised FSD without modification, specifically citing the inability of the vehicle to clear its own cameras autonomously. He expects severe limitations on any unsupervised FSD deployment in current-hardware vehicles, particularly in adverse weather.

## Robotaxi

1. **Robotaxi Fleet Vehicles Not Counted as Deliveries:** Brian addresses whether vehicles placed into a robotaxi network count as delivered. His conclusion is that they would be produced but not delivered, as there is no final customer receiving the vehicle. He draws a parallel to Megapacks deployed in-house, which are counted as deployed but not sold.
