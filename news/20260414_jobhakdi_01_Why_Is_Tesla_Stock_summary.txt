This video from Jo Bhakdi of the Pioneer/TTT investment community analyzes why Tesla stock was rallying on April 14, 2026, attributing the move primarily to visible Cybercab production and deployment momentum rather than any single headline catalyst. Bhakdi argues that despite some market disappointment over FSD v14.3 being an incremental rather than transformational update, the underlying Cybercab ramp is proceeding as predicted and the stock had fallen too far from its December 2025 highs. His core thesis is that Tesla's stock is undervalued even without unsupervised robotaxi scaling, and that the accumulation of incremental positive signals — Cybercab sightings nationwide, FSD approval in Europe, and recovering EV sales — point to a recovery toward $400 and beyond.

## Autonomous Driving
1. **FSD v14.3 Delivers Incremental Improvements, Not the Anticipated 10x Leap:** Bhakdi notes that FSD v14.3 was released and tested publicly, but Elon Musk clarified it is not the much-anticipated 10x larger model — it is an incremental improvement. Despite that framing, Bhakdi highlights that v14.3 introduces 20% improved reaction time and faster iteration cycles, which he considers meaningful progress. The market initially reacted with disappointment, putting pressure on the stock.

2. **FSD v15 Expected Within Three Months:** Bhakdi mentions that FSD version 15 is "probably coming up in the next 3 months," though he cautions against building up expectations that it will be a definitive milestone version. He suggests the transition to unsupervised operation may be more gradual than a single binary event.

3. **FSD Officially Approved and Launched in the Netherlands:** A significant regulatory milestone is highlighted: FSD supervised has been officially approved and launched in the Netherlands, making it the first European country to accept it. Bhakdi notes this approval is limited to Hardware 4 (HW4) vehicles and expresses confidence that other European countries will follow quickly once one has set the precedent. He frames this as a preparatory step toward a broader European robotaxi rollout.

## Robotaxi
1. **Cybercab Production Ramping Daily at Giga Texas:** Bhakdi references Joe Tegtmeyer's ongoing photo documentation of Cybercab production at Giga Texas in Austin, noting daily output figures of approximately 35, 45, and 60 units on successive days. He describes this as confirmation that the Cybercab ramp is proceeding as predicted in April 2026, even though unsupervised deployment has not yet scaled.

2. **Cybercab Sightings Expanding Across the United States:** Cybercabs with safety drivers and improvised steering wheels are now being spotted in an expanding list of cities beyond Austin, including Wichita (Kansas), Los Angeles, Naples (Florida), Chicago, Boston, and New York. Bhakdi frames this geographic spread as evidence that Tesla is building out test and operational infrastructure ahead of a broader unsupervised launch.

3. **Total Cybercab Fleet at 574, Unsupervised Count at Approximately 11:** Bhakdi cites a total Cybercab fleet figure of 574 vehicles, with 480 described in one context and approximately 11 operating unsupervised. He acknowledges this number changes frequently. He frames the unsupervised count as the last major uncertainty holding back the stock's repricing.

4. **Unsupervised Scale-Up Expected to Be Gradual:** Rather than a single launch event, Bhakdi argues the path to unsupervised robotaxi scale will likely be stepwise — going from roughly 11 unsupervised vehicles to 22, then 50, then 100, with pauses in between — while Tesla simultaneously builds out charging and fleet infrastructure. He considers this a healthier and more realistic framing than waiting for one decisive "go" moment.

5. **Tesla Hiring for Robotaxi Operations Nationwide:** Bhakdi mentions observing Tesla hiring employees for robotaxi operations across multiple locations, interpreting this as further evidence that a commercial rollout is being actively prepared.

## Electric Vehicles
1. **Tesla Sales Recovery and Relative Market Strength:** Bhakdi notes positive sales recovery signals for Tesla after a difficult prior year, and highlights that Tesla's market share is growing in both the United States and Europe even as the broader auto market is weak. He contrasts this with other EV makers and traditional automakers, all of which he says are declining, making Tesla's relative performance notably strong.

## Market & Competition
1. **Stock Has Fallen Too Far Relative to Fundamentals:** Bhakdi argues that Tesla's stock at approximately $360–$370 is deeply undervalued relative to where it stood in December 2025 at around $490, despite the company being fundamentally stronger today. He sets a near-term price target of $400+ without any new major catalyst, and a repricing target of $600–$800 if unsupervised robotaxi deployment scales meaningfully.

2. **Broader AI and Semiconductor Sector Tailwind:** Bhakdi expresses a bullish macro view on AI hardware and semiconductor companies, describing the current moment as a "cold AI spring" for the NASDAQ and hyperscalers, which he sees as a favorable tailwind for Tesla specifically. He notes his own portfolio was up approximately 5.9% on the day of recording.
