1. Lemonade Insurance expanding FSD discount to Oregon. Lemonade, a car insurance provider with full access to Tesla driving data, has confirmed through its own analysis that FSD drivers are two times safer than human drivers. The CEO of Lemonade previously stated that FSD insurance could eventually approach near-zero cost as the technology improves further. Joe argues Lemonade may actually be ahead of Tesla itself in understanding and monetizing FSD safety data, since insurance is their core competency. He predicts a cascade effect where major insurers like Geico and Farmers will be forced to follow suit.

2. Cybercab production is ramping at Giga Texas. Tesla's job board shows 53 new Cybercab manufacturing roles being hired, covering assembly, injection molding, and castings — all full-time in Austin. Both hosts view this as confirmation that Cybercab production launch remains on track for April 2026.

3. Production volume estimates for Cybercab are significantly higher than mainstream expectations. Joe argues that because Cybercab cannot be commercialized without FSD, the decision to scale manufacturing is itself proof that Tesla has high internal confidence in unsupervised robotaxi deployment. He uses Cybertruck as a conservative benchmark — Cybertruck scaled to 28,000 units in its first three quarters despite being more mechanically complex than Cybercab. Joe's model projects 20,000 Cybercabs as a low-end scenario and 40,000 as an ambitious end-of-2026 target, reasoning that anything significantly below 20,000 would represent a catastrophic capex and supply chain planning failure that would be unprecedented for Tesla. 4. Tesla VP of AI Ashok Alwami discussed fully integrated voice control. He stated that full voice command integration — the ability to tell the car where to park, how to drive, etc. — is coming but is too early to deploy right now. The primary obstacle is safety: extensive testing is required to ensure the system cannot be exploited through adversarial voice commands. Herbert noted that Grok and FSD are currently two separate AI systems layered on top of each other, raising the theoretical possibility that Grok could eventually serve as a supervisory check on FSD decisions.
