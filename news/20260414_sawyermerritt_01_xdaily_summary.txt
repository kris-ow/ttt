## Key Posts / Themes

1. **FSD European Regulatory Breakthrough — RDW Seeks EU-Wide Approval:** The highest-signal story of the day is that the Dutch vehicle authority (RDW) has formally notified the European Commission of its intent to seek EU-wide approval for Tesla FSD (Supervised). The next steps involve all EU member states reviewing the application, with the European Commission serving as the coordinating body. Merritt emphasized (3.1K likes, 580 reposts) that the RDW frequently sets regulatory standards that influence the rest of Europe, and that this approval pathway should accelerate FSD rollout across the continent. A video of FSD (Supervised) navigating Amsterdam's notoriously complex cycling and pedestrian environment — described as "the biking capital of the world" — was shared alongside this news, providing real-world demonstration of capability in a European context (4.1K likes, 391 reposts).

2. **Deutsche Bank Analyst Rides Tesla Robotaxi in Austin — Positive Assessment:** Deutsche Bank analyst Edison Yu provided a firsthand account of riding in a Tesla Model Y robotaxi in Austin for the first time. Yu described the technology as "impressive and seamless," specifically noting the vehicle's ability to handle merges, initiate lane change signals, and navigate dense traffic. This constitutes a credible third-party validation of the Austin robotaxi service's current operational quality from a sell-side analyst with direct institutional reach.

3. **Tesla Spring Software Update — New Self-Driving App for AI4 Hardware:** Tesla officially unveiled its Spring software update, headlined by a new Self-Driving App rolling out to all AI4 hardware vehicles where FSD is available (4.4K likes, 217 reposts — the day's most engaged Tesla-specific post). The app allows users to subscribe with a single tap, view monthly FSD usage via bar chart, track total FSD percentage, monitor multi-day streaks, and learn how to activate the feature. Merritt noted this finally provides "deeper info" on FSD usage patterns. Other update features included a Blind Spot Warning Accent Light improvement. The AI4 hardware exclusivity is a meaningful detail, reinforcing the hardware segmentation between legacy and current-gen vehicles.

4. **Delaware Court Dismisses Shareholder Lawsuits Against Tesla:** A Delaware court dismissed several shareholder lawsuits against Tesla, ruling they must be refiled in Texas. Vice Chancellor Bonnie W. David stated she would "not second-guess Tesla stockholders' chosen forum." This was characterized as a significant legal win for Tesla and Elon Musk (4.4K likes, 450 reposts), removing near-term litigation overhang from Delaware jurisdiction.

5. **U.S. Q1 2026 EV Sales — Tesla Dominates, Cybertruck Falls Sharply:** Cox Auto data for Q1 2026 showed Tesla maintaining overwhelming EV market leadership. The Model Y led all EVs with 78,591 units sold, followed by the Model 3 at 31,672. The next closest competitor was Toyota bZ at 10,029 — less than 13% of Model Y volume. However, the Cybertruck posted only 3,519 units, a steep -45% year-over-year decline, making it the 9th-ranked EV overall. Among EV pickups, the Ford F-150 Lightning was noted as discontinued. The Cybertruck's sharp decline is a notable demand concern for that specific nameplate, though Tesla's overall EV position remains dominant.

6. **Tesla Semi Canadian Debut at Truck World 2026:** Tesla is preparing to display the Tesla Semi at Truck World 2026 in Ontario, Canada, running April 16–18 — its first Canadian public display. While a relatively modest catalyst, it signals continued commercial trucking market development in North America.
