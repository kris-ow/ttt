This video provides a comprehensive global status report on Tesla's FSD rollout, arguing that the technology has crossed a critical inflection point from "promise" to "product." The host walks through every country where FSD is live, pending, or in trials, while highlighting a viral pedestrian-prediction clip as evidence of a technical breakthrough in AI-based intent modeling. The core thesis is that the market is still pricing FSD as a vehicle feature rather than a global software platform, and that April 2026 represents a convergence of catalysts — regulatory, technical, and commercial — that could trigger a significant re-rating.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **FSD Supervised live in seven countries:** FSD Supervised is currently approved and available to customers in the United States (live since 2023, with unsupervised FSD now active in geofenced areas of Austin), Canada, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea (launched November 2025). China has partial approval but remains constrained by data localization laws that prevent Tesla from transferring training videos outside the country, and the government has banned the term "Full Self-Driving," requiring Tesla to rename it "intelligent assisted driving." Elon Musk has said he expects full approval in China in 2026.

2. **South Korea's rapid adoption and performance:** South Korea launched FSD Supervised in November 2025 and has already logged 5 million FSD miles in just 100 days. The Korea Expressway Corporation rated highway performance as "excellent," making it one of the fastest-ramping international deployments on record.

3. **Abu Dhabi trials mark Middle East debut:** Supervised FSD trials on public roads launched in Abu Dhabi on March 1st under the Abu Dhabi Mobility Authority, representing the first confirmed FSD footprint in the Middle East. These are structured trials, not yet a full consumer launch.

4. **Netherlands expected to be first EU approval on April 10th:** The Dutch Vehicle Authority (RDW) has been working with Tesla on an 18-month testing program. Tesla completed 1.66 million km of testing on European roads and conducted over 13,000 customer ride-alongs. All documentation for UN Regulation 171 approval and Article 39 exemptions has been submitted. The RDW confirmed in late March that they are in the final internal review stage, with an expected decision date of April 10th. The host is careful to note this is an expected date, not a confirmed approval.

5. **Netherlands approval could cascade to 27 EU countries:** Under EU Regulation 2018/858, once the Netherlands grants type approval, other EU member states can recognize that approval through a mutual recognition process, potentially opening the door to all 27 EU countries without each running a full independent review. Tesla anticipates a coordinated EU-wide rollout by summer, covering Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, Austria, and the Nordic countries. Tesla has been running FSD demonstration ride-alongs across Germany, France, Italy, Denmark, and Switzerland through March 31st, extending the program multiple times due to popularity.

6. **Israel and Japan are next in the pipeline:** Israel's Transport Minister publicly stated FSD is "coming soon," following government approval of supervised autonomous driving trials on public roads in February. In Japan, Tesla's Japan President Hiroshi Hashimoto confirmed FSD is targeting a launch this year, with active testing underway in Tokyo using both Model Y and Model 3 vehicles. If approved, an over-the-air update could instantly activate FSD for approximately 40,000 Teslas already on Japanese roads.

7. **FSD version 14.3 described as "the last big piece of the puzzle":** Elon Musk confirmed on March 19th that FSD version 14.3 is in testing, with a wide release expected in early to mid-April 2026. Elon described it as adding an order-of-magnitude larger neural network with more reasoning capabilities and reinforcement learning. He described the expected result as making the car feel "almost like a sentient being." This version is characterized as completing the core architecture rather than being an incremental update.

8. **Pedestrian intent prediction clip signals architectural breakthrough:** A viral clip published through Tesla's official account showed the FSD system slowing and yielding for a pedestrian who was standing on the sidewalk — not yet moving toward the road — and then the pedestrian stepped off the curb seconds later, exactly as anticipated. The host explains this is made possible by FSD version 14's end-to-end neural network architecture, where perception, prediction, and planning are unified rather than separate modules. The system reads body language cues — orientation, head direction, weight shift — to model likely intent rather than simply reacting to detected movement.

9. **Safety data trending toward 10x human safety:** Tesla's Q2 2025 safety report showed FSD Supervised vehicles were involved in approximately one crash every 7.6 million miles driven, compared to the national average of roughly one crash every 670,000 miles for human drivers — placing current FSD at roughly seven times safer on a per-mile basis. Elon posted on X that Tesla AI self-driving "will be" greater than 10 times safer than human driving, which the host clarifies is a forward-looking statement, not a current confirmed statistic. The host acknowledges legitimate methodological debates: Tesla's numbers are self-reported, not independently verified, and FSD may operate more frequently in favorable conditions. Tesla's total accumulated training data stands at 8.4 billion miles.

10. **Narrative shift among skeptics and media:** Motor Trend named FSD Supervised the winner of their best driver assistance system award for 2026, with editors explicitly acknowledging their previous skepticism and writing that "the majority of the flaws we noted in earlier versions have largely been addressed." At CES 2026, industry analysts noted that the entire autonomous driving industry has converged on Tesla-like vision-based solutions, with one analyst calling it "an admission that alternative paths were dead ends." Germany's biggest car magazine tested FSD Supervised and called it "genuinely stunning." NHTSA is noted as actively investigating FSD's performance.

## Robotaxi

1. **Austin robotaxi service accumulating incident data:** The Austin robotaxi service — currently operating in geofenced areas with unsupervised FSD — has had roughly 14 reported incidents over 18 months. The host presents this alongside the broader limitations disclosure, noting that fully unsupervised FSD remains limited to small geofenced boundaries in just one city.

2. **Cybercab production begins in April:** Cybercab production is noted as beginning in April 2026, coinciding with the broader cluster of FSD catalysts. Tesla is also hiring AI safety operators in 18 US cities across 12 states, which the host reads as preparation for expanding the unsupervised robotaxi footprint beyond Austin.

3. **Geofence expansion is the key signal to watch:** The host identifies the expansion of the unsupervised FSD zone beyond Austin as the critical indicator that a nationwide robotaxi rollout is accelerating. When new cities are added to the unsupervised zone, that signals the inflection from pilot to platform.

## Market & Competition

1. **Waymo constrained; Tesla's global reach is unmatched:** The host notes that Waymo operates in only a handful of US cities and that Cruise is effectively paused, while Tesla is pursuing simultaneous global expansion across seven approved markets and multiple pending ones. No other autonomous driving system has the same fleet reach for an over-the-air activation model.

2. **Industry convergence on Tesla's camera-only approach:** At CES 2026, the broader autonomous driving industry was noted to have converged on Tesla-like vision-based solutions, validating Tesla's long-contested camera-only architecture after years of competitors mocking the approach.

At $99 to $199 per month per subscriber across a fleet growing toward 10 million vehicles, a full global FSD subscription rollout could generate a software revenue stream the host compares to entire SaaS companies. Q1 2026 earnings in late April are flagged as the next financial data point to watch, specifically FSD take rate, subscription revenue, and forward guidance on unsupervised expansion.
