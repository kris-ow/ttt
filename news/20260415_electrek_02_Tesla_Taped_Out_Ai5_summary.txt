Tesla has taped out its AI5 self-driving chip, CEO Elon Musk announced on X at 3:21 AM on April 15, 2026 — a meaningful milestone, but one that arrives nearly two years behind the company's original timeline and still leaves volume production more than a year away.

## Autonomous Driving
1. **AI5 tape-out milestone and what it means:** Tape-out locks the chip design and sends it to the semiconductor foundry for fabrication, but manufacturing, silicon testing, validation, and production ramp typically take 12–18 months for an automotive-grade AI accelerator. Tesla is reportedly using TSMC for AI5 production. Musk has described AI5 as up to 10x more powerful than AI4. A "AI4.5" stopgap compute platform was quietly introduced in 2026 Model Y vehicles to bridge the gap while AI5 slipped.

2. **A timeline of repeated delays:** Tesla's AI5 promises have shifted repeatedly: Musk said in June 2024 it would be in vehicles in the "second half of 2025"; in July 2025 he said the design was "finished"; in November 2025 Tesla pushed volume production to mid-2027; in January 2026 Musk said the design was "almost done"; and tape-out was confirmed April 15, 2026. Volume production remains expected no earlier than mid-2027.

3. **AI6 also slipping:** The follow-on AI6 chip, tied to Samsung's 2nm fabrication line, has already slipped roughly six months due to Samsung 2nm yield issues, pushing mass production to Q4 2027 at the earliest. Musk also referenced Dojo3 and other chips as being in development.

## Robotaxi
1. **Cybercab launches on AI4, not AI5:** The Cybercab, scheduled for production in Q2 2026, will launch on current-generation AI4 hardware — the same platform used in Model Y, Model 3, Model S, Model X, and Cybertruck. Tesla has stated it needs "several hundred thousand completed AI5 boards line side" before switching production lines, a threshold not expected until mid-2027.

## Market & Competition
1. **Terafab chip manufacturing project:** In March 2026, Tesla and SpaceX announced the $25 billion "Terafab" project in Austin. In April 2026, Intel joined Terafab to fabricate and package the chips.

## Editorial Stance
Author Fred Lambert argues that repeated chip generation announcements follow a troubling pattern: each new chip renders the previous one obsolete before Tesla has delivered on promises made to customers who purchased vehicles on earlier hardware (HW3, HW4). Lambert contends the tape-out is genuine progress, but is simultaneously a "quiet confirmation that a lot of existing customers are not getting the product they paid for on the hardware they own."
