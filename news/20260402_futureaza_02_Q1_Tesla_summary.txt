## Electric Vehicles

1. **Q1 2026 Official Results — Delivery Miss, Production Near Estimates:** Tesla reported Q1 2026 total deliveries of 358,023 vehicles against production of 408,577 vehicles. Brian had personally estimated 382,000 deliveries and 392,000 produced, meaning he missed deliveries by a wide margin but production by only about 2,611 units (roughly half a percent). The Wall Street analyst consensus was approximately 365,645 deliveries, making the actual result a meaningful miss even against the Street.

2. **Large Production-Delivery Gap Raises Questions:** The roughly 50,000-unit gap between production and deliveries — with more units produced than delivered — drew viewer discussion about whether surplus inventory is being held for the nascent robotaxi fleet, directed into a rental fleet, or simply represents vehicles in transit. Brian acknowledged the gap is unusual but characterized the energy storage deployment shortfall similarly — as inventory that will flow through in future quarters.

3. **Giga Texas Shutdown — Model Y Long (Y L) Retooling:** Brian expressed high confidence that the Giga Texas production shutdown during the week of the report is related to Model Y Long (Y L) retooling. He noted that most of the tooling work was already completed in a prior worldwide factory shutdown last year, and that Y L production movement has also been observed at Giga Berlin. He pushed back on critics who say the Model Y isn't big enough, pointing out it remains the world's best-selling EV despite four factory shutdowns for retooling globally.

4. **Model Y Global Sales Performance — Regional Data Points:** Third-party monthly data cited by analyst Roland Percher gave Brian additional pre-release confidence in his estimates. South Korea reported approximately 11,000 Tesla sales in March alone, making Tesla the third best-selling brand behind Hyundai and Kia. Giga Shanghai wholesale figures came in at approximately 85,000 units in March. Italy reported approximately 2,920 sales for March. European weekly sales of approximately 6,400 were also noted. Spain and France were described as performing well in Q1.

5. **SNX (Model S/X) Discontinuation — Inventory Clearing:** Brian noted that SNX production has ended, with only limited remaining inventory. He advised viewers who have been waiting to buy a Model S or Model X to act quickly before remaining units sell out.

## Energy

1. **Q1 Energy Deployment — Below Expectations:** Energy storage deployment came in at 8.8 gigawatt hours for Q1 2026. This was below the sell-side analyst consensus estimate of 14.4 gigawatt hours, and well below the 15 gigawatt hours one viewer had predicted. Brian characterized the shortfall as a "spinning flywheel" — not a structural problem, but a timing issue that should result in a larger Q2 figure given continued production throughput.

## Robotaxi

1. **Cybercab Fleet Classification — Internal vs. Customer Deliveries:** Brian addressed how Cybercab units will appear in future Tesla delivery reports. His view is that Cybercabs kept internally for the robotaxi fleet will not count as customer deliveries; they will appear as surplus production. Customer-sold Cybercabs would show in the "other" vehicle category, which previously included Cybertruck but will now shift toward Semi and Cybercab. He does not expect large numbers of Cybercabs to show up as customer deliveries initially.

2. **Cybercab Public Rides — Timeline Uncertain:** Brian was asked when he expects Cybercab to begin public unsupervised rides. He declined to give a firm date (this month or next), saying he had previously expected the launch to follow the removal of safety drivers, but that this has not yet triggered a public launch announcement. He is still watching for that milestone.

## Financials

1. **Delivery Miss Context and Stock Reaction:** Tesla shares were already trading down in pre-market prior to the number release. Brian noted this could have reflected either foreknowledge of the weak delivery number or the broader market reaction to a Trump tariff speech from the prior day. The stock was reported down approximately 4.2% on a year-over-year basis at the time of the stream. Brian reiterated he does not trade around delivery numbers given the unpredictability of the market's reaction — sometimes the stock goes down on a beat and up on a miss.
