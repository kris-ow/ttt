A Dutch Tesla owner who paid €6,400 for FSD in 2019 called Tesla to ask when HW3 cars would receive FSD functionality. After seven years, Tesla's response was to "just be patient" — with no timeline, no retrofit program, and no refund policy offered.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **Tesla's HW3 FSD deadlock in Europe:** Mischa Sigtermans, who purchased FSD on a Model 3 in the Netherlands in 2019, called Tesla after the Dutch vehicle authority RDW granted Tesla type approval for FSD Supervised — the first EU approval — only for AI4 hardware. HW3 owners like Sigtermans are excluded entirely. Tesla's phone agent stated there was "no information about when it comes, or if it comes at all," no information about a free hardware upgrade "within Europe," and no information about how Tesla plans to handle EU HW3 owners. After the call, Tesla closed Sigtermans' case and sent an automated email with a link to book a test drive.

2. **Timeline of broken promises:** When FSD was sold in 2019, Tesla represented it as achievable via software updates on existing hardware. By August 2024, Tesla VP of AI Ashok Elluswamy acknowledged HW3 runs a "relatively smaller model" than AI4 with workarounds. In January 2025, on the Q4 2024 earnings call, Elon Musk admitted Tesla would "need to replace all HW3 computers in vehicles where FSD was purchased," calling it "painful and difficult" and saying he was "kind of glad that not that many people bought the FSD package." Tesla has filed a patent describing a workaround to run a modern FSD model on HW3, which the patent itself acknowledges can render the system "inoperable." Tesla has vaguely promised a "v14 Lite" for HW3 in Q2 2026, described in the article as a fundamentally stripped-down product distinct from what was originally sold.

## Market & Competition

3. **Growing EU collective legal action:** Sigtermans launched hw3claim.nl to bundle HW3 + FSD owners into a collective EU claim seeking €6,800 per owner. Within one week, 3,000 owners from 29 countries signed up, representing over €6 million in FSD purchases. The article notes EU consumer protection law is significantly stronger than US law, with robust collective-redress frameworks in the Netherlands, Germany, and France. A separate class-action was filed in Australia in October 2025 following Musk's HW3 admission.

## Editorial Stance
The author argues Tesla has no answer — not a bad answer, but no answer — and that Tesla's own CEO admission and patent filings constitute a damaging paper trail. The author states they are "increasingly convinced this will end up in court" and that the "be patient" response will look damaging before a European judge.
