## Key Posts / Themes

1. **TERAFAB Announcement – Tesla/SpaceX/xAI Joint Chip Fab at Giga Texas**
Elon Musk announced that Tesla and SpaceX will build an advanced technology fabrication facility at Giga Texas capable of producing chips end-to-end — from lithography masks to testing — in a single building. The fab will produce two kinds of chips. This is a significant vertical integration play that could reduce Tesla's dependence on third-party semiconductor suppliers (e.g., NVIDIA, TSMC) and strengthen its AI infrastructure stack long-term.

2. **Tesla Dojo 3 Chips Powering SpaceX AI Satellites**
Tesla's D3 chip has been confirmed inside SpaceX's mini AI satellites, which are planned to be deployed via an electromagnetic mass driver on the Moon. This represents a direct revenue/utilization pathway for Tesla's in-house silicon beyond vehicles and FSD, and validates the Dojo program's commercial scalability.

3. **SpaceX AI Satellites – Scale and Cost Trajectory**
Musk stated that the cost of deploying AI in space will fall below terrestrial AI deployment costs within 2–3 years, with satellites scaling from 100kW to the megawatt range. If Tesla chips are a core component of this buildout, the addressable market for Tesla's silicon expands dramatically beyond automotive.

4. **Tesla/SpaceX Merger Speculation**
Merritt posted that a Tesla and SpaceX merger "feels inevitable at this point." While speculative, this sentiment — coming amid a joint fab announcement with a unified TERAFAB logo (Tesla + SpaceX + xAI) — is likely to fuel investor debate around corporate structure, valuation, and Elon's attention allocation. A merger would be one of the most consequential corporate events in Tesla's history.

5. **Full TERAFAB Presentation Posted**
Merritt shared the full Elon Musk TERAFAB presentation video, making it widely accessible. High engagement (1.1K+ likes on key posts) suggests strong retail investor interest and broad narrative momentum around Tesla's role in the AI/space chip ecosystem.
