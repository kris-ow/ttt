This video from the "brighterwithherbert" channel covers Tesla's landmark approval of FSD Supervised in the Netherlands and the immediate push by Dutch regulators to seek EU-wide approval, which the host and guest Jeff (a multi-decade supply chain executive and manufacturing consultant) argue is a pivotal moment for Tesla's global autonomous driving expansion. The core thesis is that the Netherlands approval is not just a single-country win but a gateway to broader European and international FSD adoption, laying critical groundwork for the eventual robotaxi rollout. The video also covers ongoing robotaxi hiring in Texas, a new Optimus charging job posting, and two recently rolled-out vehicle software features.

## Autonomous Driving

1. **Netherlands FSD Supervised Approval — First in Europe:** Tesla's FSD Supervised received regulatory approval in the Netherlands from the RDW (Dutch Vehicle Authority) for the first time in Europe. The host notes that the RDW conducted 18 months of rigorous testing and concluded that FSD would make roads net safer. Elon Musk publicly congratulated the team and acknowledged the regulators were "extremely rigorous" in their review. The version approved in Europe is designated 2026.3.6 in local naming conventions, but Elon confirmed it is the equivalent of FSD version 14.3.

2. **Push for EU-Wide Approval:** The general manager of the RDW announced just two days after the Netherlands approval that the Dutch Vehicle Authority plans to notify the European Commission and seek EU-wide approval for Tesla's FSD Supervised. A majority vote among responsible EU member states is required for approval to pass. The host and guest note that the RDW is widely recognized as a standard-setter that influences almost all of Europe, and that approval could extend beyond the 27 EU member states to additional countries that follow European regulation — a point confirmed by Ashok Elluswami on X.

3. **Timeline for Broader European Approvals:** Jeff estimates that additional country approvals could begin flowing in within the next 4 to 8 weeks, with end of summer as a worst-case scenario. He emphasizes that no additional hardware is required — existing Tesla vehicles in Europe already have the necessary hardware and only need a software update. Tesla has also already sent customers a tutorial via email and embedded one in the vehicle software, which must be played before FSD can be used.

4. **FSD Performance in European Conditions:** Early customer videos from the Netherlands, including footage in Amsterdam city center, showed FSD navigating narrow roads, heavy cyclist and pedestrian traffic, roundabouts, and road works. Elon confirmed that Tesla's self-driving handles Dutch roundabouts well. Jeff highlighted that FSD version 14.3 has significantly faster reaction times than prior versions, as documented in Tesla's own release notes, and compared the system's eight-camera 360-degree coverage favorably to the limitations of human vision.

5. **Cross-Border Warnings and European-Specific UI Features:** When crossing into a country where FSD is not yet approved, drivers receive a 15-second advance warning to take over. The host noted that European FSD also includes a mandatory video tutorial before first use and a more detailed FSD usage tracking UI — features the host described as potentially ahead of the US experience. He drew a parallel to how similar state-border warnings may function in the US once unsupervised FSD is approved in some states but not others.

6. **Global Regulatory Ripple Effect:** Jeff argued that Europe's approval — from one of the world's toughest regulatory environments — will encourage other countries to either conduct their own testing with a higher degree of confidence or simply accept the European approval report as sufficient. Ashok noted that European regulation is followed by many more countries beyond the 27 EU member states. The host also flagged China as the next major expected approval, noting there are over a million Tesla vehicles already in China.

7. **Safety Data and the Path to Unsupervised FSD:** The guest referenced nearly 10 billion miles of FSD data captured, particularly on more recent releases, as evidence that the system is many times safer than human driving. He framed the European supervised approval as the essential foundation for eventually obtaining unmanned/unsupervised approvals in Europe and globally, arguing that regulators who have seen FSD perform well in supervised mode will be more receptive to future unsupervised applications.

## Robotaxi

1. **Robotaxi Field Response Specialist Hiring Ramp in Texas:** Tesla is actively ramping up hiring of robotaxi field response specialists across Austin, Houston, and Dallas, Texas, with day, afternoon, and night shifts being filled. The job involves immediate on-road support for autonomous vehicle incidents, ensuring rider safety, conducting basic roadside repairs, assisting first responders, and aiding vehicle recovery. The host argued this hiring pattern is consistent with an imminent commercial robotaxi service launch, not merely expanded testing.

2. **Debate Over Robotaxi Launch Readiness:** Jeff acknowledged that milestones discussed at the end of January 2026 (including doubling deployments every month) have not been met, but pushed back on narratives linking the delay to a requirement for FSD version 15. He argued the pace of job postings does not suggest a slowdown and that Tesla is likely between two software releases while validating safety performance. Both host and guest expressed a desire to see new supervised robotaxi cities launched this month (April 2026), with concern that remaining at just Austin and the Bay Area through the end of April would be a negative signal.

3. **Pre-Earnings Robotaxi and Cybercab Announcements Anticipated:** The guest suggested Tesla may time a new city rollout or a Cybercab production start announcement prior to the April 22, 2026 earnings call to give management positive milestones to discuss. He noted that analysts will likely press management on why the January 29th guidance of doubling deployments monthly has not been achieved and what is different now. The host speculated that Giga Berlin — following the union's recent loss — could potentially be announced as a Cybercab and Optimus production site, given Elon's earlier comments about making it Europe's largest factory.

4. **Long-Term Vision: Automation of Field Response:** Jeff noted that while Tesla is hiring significant numbers of human field response staff now, the long-term expectation is that these manual processes will be progressively automated using AI, with human staffing scaled to satisfy regulatory and local government requirements during the transition period.
